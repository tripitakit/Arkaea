# Arkea — Inception & Design Document

## Context

**Arkea** is a game/simulation of evolution of proto-bacterial organisms (the *Arkeons*). The project's goal is to create an experience that is:

- **Scientifically accurate**, addressed to an audience of biologists, microbiologists, geneticists and molecular biologists
- A **web app with 2D WebGL UI**
- Centered on the creation of an Arkeon: the player defines its **cellular structure** (membrane, wall, integrated proteins) and **genome** (structural and enzymatic genes for metabolism and replication)
- Capable of simulating **mobile genetic elements** (plasmids, prophages) for the horizontal transfer of traits, metabolic pathways and resistances

The guiding constraint is: **simplify by several orders of magnitude** the complexity of a real bacterial genome, **without trivializing the biology**. The expert audience must recognize real mechanisms (operons, σ-factor regulation, riboswitch, integrases, point mutagenesis, rearrangements) and be able to reason with their own expertise.

This document consolidates the scope decisions taken in the first brainstorming sessions and remains the design reference from which to derive subsequent implementation plans.

## Consolidated decisions

### Block 1 — World architecture

| Axis | Choice |
|---|---|
| Genre | Evolutionary sandbox + strategic-competitive ecosystem (hybrid) |
| Persistence | Persistent world, 24/7 server-side simulation |
| Multiplayer | **Single shared world (MMO-like)**: all players populate the same ecosystem |
| Time | **Continuous accelerated even offline**: the ecosystem evolves regardless of player presence |
| Player role | **Designer + Breeder/engineer**: designs the initial Arkeon and then actively intervenes (selection, plasmid introduction, targeted genomic modifications) |

**Known tensions deferred to a later phase:**
- Behavior of the player's Arkeon when offline (possible extinction? stasis? AI autopilot?)
- Onboarding of new players in an already evolved world
- Anti-griefing and competitive balancing
- Infrastructural costs of the 24/7 simulation
- Sharding/scaling as the player population grows

### Block 2 — Granularity of the biological model

**Choice: "B+C" level — modular with sub-genic touches.**

#### Genome
- **Chromosome** = ordered sequence of **genes** (discrete objects) grouped in **operons**
- Each gene has:
  - **Functional identity** generated by an **abstract/generative system** (functions emerge from composable parameters, not from a finite predefined catalog)
  - **Quantitative parameters** (Km, Vmax, affinity, stability) — substrate of selection
  - **Short symbolic sequence** (~50-200 logical codons, not real ATGC) sufficient to support point mutations, recombination hotspots, integration sites
- **Plasmids and prophages** = separate genomic fragments with their own dynamics (replication, loss, chromosomal integration, excision)

#### Gene regulation (chosen depth)
- **Operons** with on/off promoters modulated by **environmental metabolites** (induction/repression)
- **Sigma factor** for global transcriptional programs (e.g. stress response, sporulation-like, stationary phase)
- **Riboswitch** for fast post-transcriptional regulation (e.g. direct binding to metabolite)

#### Supported mutations
- Point (logical codon substitutions)
- Indels
- Gene duplications (substrate for neofunctionalization)
- **Large rearrangements**: inversions and translocations

#### Metabolism
- 8-15 key metabolites as "chemical currency" (exact set to be defined in Block 3)
- Reactions catalyzed by enzymes encoded in the genome, simplified Michaelis-Menten kinetics
- ATP-equivalent balance as a central constraint (replication, protein synthesis, active transport cost)
- Steady-state proto-FBA recomputed per tick (not molecular simulation)

#### Membrane and wall
- **Membrane**: lipid composition (fluidity, permeability) + integral proteins (porins, transporters, receptors, efflux pumps)
- **Wall**: type (absent / Gram-like thin / Gram-like thick / S-layer) → metabolic cost + resistance to osmotic stress, lysis, phages
- It is the main **visible phenotype** on the 2D WebGL UI

#### Deliberate exclusions
- No real ribosomes/translation (genes produce enzymes at a parametric rate)
- No real replisome (division = event with cost + error rate)
- No complex intracellular structures (no compartments, no cytoskeleton)
- No real ATGC DNA (more abstract symbolic sequence)

**Rationale of the generative system for functions**: a finite catalog (e.g. 200 predefined KEGG families) would be more readable but would close the evolutionary space. A generative system (composable parameters that produce new functions under selective pressure) preserves the open nature of evolution, at the cost of greater complexity in modeling.

### Block 3 — Environment and selective pressure

#### Spatiality — hybrid two-scale model

**Macroscale**: the world is a graph of **multiple connected biotopes**. Each biotope is a local chemostat with its own chemistry (metabolite concentrations), physical parameters (temperature, pH, osmolarity), and Arkeon population. **Migration** between biotopes happens via parametric flows/dispersion. This is the scale on which the 24/7 server-side simulation runs.

**Microscale**: when a player observes a biotope up close, a **local continuous 2D representation** of the same population and chemistry is presented. The microscale is predominantly for **visualization/interaction** (detailed physics is not authoritative for the global simulation, but the player sees gradients, biofilms, colonies). This allows for rich WebGL UI without paying the cost of 2D diffusion everywhere.

**Consequences:**
- Ecological niches are global (per biotope) but visually recognizable
- The simulation cost scales with the number of biotopes, not with the visualized area
- The design must clarify what of the microscale is authoritative (e.g. local player events?) and what is purely decorative

#### Events and perturbations — system + player on their own biotope

- **Systemic events**: seasonal cycles, random perturbations, natural evolution of phages and other mobile elements, nutrient arrivals
- **Player interventions**: limited to the **own biotope** — can introduce antibiotics, regulate nutrients, inoculate strains, release plasmids, modify physical parameters
- **No direct PvP**: players cannot perturb others' biotopes. Competition between players occurs **indirectly** via:
  - Migration of Arkeons and mobile elements between connected biotopes
  - Evolutionary success of one's own strains in the network's biotopes
  - Possible ecological "leaderboard" (niche occupation, dominance, diversity)

This choice drastically reduces anti-griefing requirements and keeps the game accessible, while preserving genuine ecological interaction via inter-biotope flows.

#### Sub-themes still to be defined (deferred to dedicated exercises)

1. **Chemical inventory**: 8-15 key metabolites (glucose, NH₃, O₂, lactate, H₂S, PO₄, Fe²⁺...). To be anchored to real microbiology.
2. **Selective pressures**: mechanisms of death and loss (osmotic lysis, starvation, phage predation, antibiosis, competition, dilution).
3. **Network topology of biotopes**: number, connections, flow rules, environmental heterogeneity.
4. **What of the microscale is authoritative** vs purely visualization.

### Block 4 — Population model

**Choice: polymorphic population with per-lineage tracking (lineage-based modeling).**

The base unit is the **lineage** (a distinct genotype + an abundance), not the cell. Each biotope maintains a **lineage forest** each one with complete genome, abundance Nᵢ, fitness wᵢ, pointer to parent → reconstructable phylogenetic tree.

#### Tick dynamics (per biotope)

1. **Differential growth** of the lineage dependent on fitness × environment − metabolic costs − death rate − dilution
2. **Mutation**: probability µ at each division (modulated by stress σ-factor) → a new daughter lineage is born
3. **HGT**: probabilistic events of conjugation/transduction/transformation between lineages of the biotope (including migrants) → new lineage with acquired mobile element
4. **Migration**: fraction of abundance flows to neighboring biotopes maintaining the genotype
5. **Pruning**: lineages with N below threshold removed from the local forest (but preserved in the global phylogenetic tree)

#### Genomic storage — delta encoding

- Reference genome for each founder strain
- Each lineage stores only a **delta** (list of mutations relative to the reference of its own clade), like VCF
- Large rearrangements may consolidate a new reference if the delta becomes excessive

#### Operational caps (decided 2026-04-25)

- **Lineages cap per biotope**: **1,000**
- **Policy at cap reach**: **extinction of the rarest lineages** (the least abundant are eliminated from the local forest)
- **Historical phylogenetic tree**: **periodic decimation** (keep only ramifications of interest, e.g. those that reached a historical maximum abundance above threshold)

#### Implications on UI and DB

- **UI**: aggregation by phenotypic cluster (1,000 lineages are not individually readable); optional phylogenetic view; highlight of player's lineages and dominant ones
- **DB**: table `lineages(id, biotope_id, parent_id, abundance, delta_genome, fitness_cache)`; historical tree in separate table, periodically decimated

### Block 5 — Mechanisms of the biological engine

**Unifying principle**: *everything is metabolism* (including drugs) and *everything is encoded in the genome* (including conjugation, mutation rate, costs). No special mechanism "above" the biological model.

#### Xenobiotics as special metabolites (target → drug → effect)

Each drug/xenobiotic is a metabolite of the chemical model, with concentration in the biotope, transport via encoded porins/efflux, and possible enzymatic degradation (e.g. β-lactamase → hydrolysis). In addition it has:
- **`target_class`** (e.g. `pbp_like`, `ribosome_like`, `dna_polymerase_like`, `membrane`, `efflux_pump`)
- **`affinity`** (Kd toward the target)
- **`mode`** (cidal vs static; mechanism: blocks-synthesis, perforates-membrane, induces-mismutations…)

When present, it binds the target proteins of the lineage with rate ∝ `[drug] × [target] / Kd`. The bound target is non-functional → consequence dependent on the target. The "MIC" emerges from the amount of free target the lineage maintains.

**Consequence**: no separate "antibiotics" system. A single framework covers β-lactams, aminoglycoside-likes, fluoroquinolone-likes, etc., and resistance evolution emerges from the same mechanisms that handle every metabolism.

#### Modulable mutation rate (SOS-like)

Each lineage has a current µ:

```
µ = µ_baseline × Π (1 + α_i × expr(repair_gene_i))
```

where the `repair_gene_i` are encoded repair/fidelity genes (`mutS_like`, `dnaQ_like`, etc.). In addition, stress σ-factor can activate an **error-prone polymerase** (DinB/Pol-V-like) that raises µ directly. **The mutation rate is an evolvable phenotype** — mutator strains emerge naturally.

#### Gene-encoded conjugation

A plasmid is conjugative if and only if it carries `pili_like + relaxase_like + oriT_like`. Conjugation rate between donor D and recipient R in the same biotope:

```
rate = expr(pili_D) × Nᴅ × Nʀ × compatibility(D, R)
```

`compatibility` depends on the recipient's surface receptors, entry-exclusion, etc. Non-conjugative plasmids move by transformation (free DNA) or mobilization through helper plasmid.

#### Plasmid cost

Two components, no "slots" (the "slot cost" is an artifact of representation, not biology):
- **Replication**: ATP proportional to plasmid size (number of genes × cost per gene), paid at each division
- **Transcriptional burden**: each tick, sum of expression of plasmid genes × cost per protein (amino-acid-eq / ATP)

Large and strongly expressed plasmids are costly → natural trade-off: useful under selective pressure, lost by dilution when pressure ceases.

#### Migration between biotopes

**Continuous drip as default**. For each arc A → B and each lineage i:

```
ΔNᵢ_migrated = base_flow × edge_weight × (Nᵢ_A / N_total_A) × biotope_compatibility
```

`base_flow` global; `edge_weight` reflects connectivity (narrow channel vs broad flow); `biotope_compatibility` reduces migration between very different environments.

**Discrete events (Poisson)** reserved for rare phenomena: free phage transport, mass dispersion (airborne), catastrophic events.

#### Generative system for gene functions — discontinuous innovations

Gene functions emerge from a system **with domain composition**, not from a finite catalog nor from continuous parameters alone:

1. **Mixed discrete+continuous function space**: each protein has a descriptor `(categorical_functional_domain, continuous_parameters)`. Point mutations typically change continuous parameters (fine tuning). Mutations in "structural" codons can **flip the categorical_functional_domain** — rare but possible events (e.g. change of substrate specificity in a transporter).
2. **Fusion/scission via large rearrangements**: a translocation that fuses two genes produces a chimeric protein with genuinely new function (combination of domains). It is the main mechanism of real evolutionary innovation, and the model explicitly supports it.

**Consequence**: the model allows three regimes of evolution: parametric drift (tuning), specificity jumps (domain change), composite innovation (fusion). All emerge from the same mutation system.

### Block 6 — Metabolic inventory

**Choice: 13 metabolites** anchored to real microbiology, designed to support distinct ecological niches and interconnected biogeochemical cycles (C, N, S, Fe).

| # | Metabolite | Roles |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Glucose** (sugar-eq, CHO_org) | C/energy substrate (electron donor), heterotrophy |
| 2 | **Acetate** | Substrate + fermentation product, key cross-feeding |
| 3 | **Lactate** | Fermentation product + substrate for others; high concentration → acidifies |
| 4 | **CO₂** | Universal product + fixable by autotrophs; ↑ slightly acidifies |
| 5 | **CH₄** (methane) | Methanogenesis product (H₂ + CO₂) + substrate for methanotrophs (with O₂) |
| 6 | **H₂** (hydrogen) | Inorganic donor for chemolithotrophs and methanogens |
| 7 | **O₂** | Aerobic acceptor; toxic for obligate anaerobes (induces σ-factor stress) |
| 8 | **NH₃/NH₄⁺** | N source + inorganic donor (nitrifiers) |
| 9 | **NO₃⁻** | Anaerobic acceptor + N source (denitrification → gaseous N₂) |
| 10 | **H₂S** | Inorganic donor + S source; toxic at high concentrations (inhibits cytochromes) |
| 11 | **SO₄²⁻** | Anaerobic acceptor + S source (reducers → H₂S, sulfur cycle) |
| 12 | **Fe²⁺ ⇌ Fe³⁺** (redox pair) | Acceptor (Fe³⁺) + cofactor + donor (Fe²⁺); supports iron reducers and oxidizers |
| 13 | **PO₄³⁻** | Essential nutrient (nucleic acids, phospholipids); often limiting in oligotrophic biotopes |

**Deliberate exclusions (reserve for future extension):**
- Free amino acids and peptides (auxotrophies, amino-acidic cross-feeding)
- Vitamins / organic cofactors (B12-like, biotin-like, growth factors)

**ATP-eq** is not in this list: it is the *internal* energy currency of the lineage (Block 2), not an environmental concentration.

#### Supported metabolic strategies (≥10 distinct niches)

| Strategy | Simplified equation | Typical niche |
|---|---|---|
| Aerobic heterotroph | Glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + ATP (high yield) | Oxygenated organic biotope |
| Fermenter | Glucose → Lactate/Acetate + ATP (low yield) | Anaerobic organic |
| Denitrifier | Glucose + NO₃⁻ → CO₂ + N₂ + ATP | Anaerobic nitric |
| Sulfate reducer | Glucose (or H₂) + SO₄²⁻ → H₂S + ATP | Anaerobic sulfurous |
| Iron reducer | Glucose + Fe³⁺ → CO₂ + Fe²⁺ + ATP | Anaerobic ferrous |
| **Methanogen** | H₂ + CO₂ → CH₄ + ATP (or acetate → CH₄ + CO₂) | Strict anaerobic |
| **Methanotroph** | CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + ATP | Oxic/anoxic interface |
| Aerobic hydrogenotroph | H₂ + O₂ → H₂O + ATP | Chemolithotrophy, autotrophy with CO₂ |
| Sulfide oxidizer | H₂S + O₂ → SO₄²⁻ + ATP | Oxic/anoxic interface, hydrothermal |
| Nitrifier | NH₃ + O₂ → NO₃⁻ + ATP | Aerobic oligotrophic |
| Iron oxidizer | Fe²⁺ + O₂ → Fe³⁺ + ATP | Acidophiles, acid drainage |

Closed cycles: **C** (CO₂↔CH₄, glucose↔acetate/lactate↔CO₂), **N** (NH₃↔NO₃⁻↔N₂), **S** (SO₄²⁻↔H₂S), **Fe** (Fe²⁺↔Fe³⁺). Niches are interdependent — products of one feed another → emergent cross-feeding.

#### Environmental parameters (not metabolites, but biotope state)

- **Temperature**: modulates kinetic k; species-specific tolerance ranges (psychrophile/mesophile/thermophile/hyperthermophile)
- **pH**: *emergent* from concentrations — CO₂↑, lactate↑, H₂S↑ acidify; NH₃↑ alkalinizes. Species-specific tolerance ranges
- **Osmolarity**: sum of solutes; impacts osmotic lysis and wall choice

These three parameters allow *physically* distinct biotopes (acid-hot hydrothermal vs cold-alkaline lake) in addition to chemically, multiplying the niches.

### Block 7 — Generative system of functional domains

#### Gene structure

A gene is an ordered sequence of **logical codons** (50–200 symbols), over an alphabet of **20 symbols** (analogous to amino acids). Codons group into consecutive blocks, each of which encodes a **functional domain**:

```
gene = [ promoter_block ] [ regulatory_block (optional) ] domain_1 domain_2 ... domain_N
```

Each domain:
- **Type tag** (first ~3 codons): categorically identifies the type
- **Parameter codons** (rest, 10–30 codons): encode the continuous parameters

Typical length: 2–10 domains per gene, consistent with real proteins.

#### Domain type taxonomy (11)

| # | Type | Key continuous parameters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Substrate-binding pocket** | target_metabolite_id, Km, specificity_breadth |
| 2 | **Catalytic site** | reaction_class (hyd/ox/red/isom/lig/lyase), kcat, cofactor |
| 3 | **Transmembrane anchor** | hydrophobicity, n_passes |
| 4 | **Channel/pore** | selectivity, gating_threshold |
| 5 | **Energy-coupling site** | atp_cost, pmf_coupling |
| 6 | **DNA-binding** | promoter_specificity, affinity |
| 7 | **Regulator output** | mode (activator/repressor), cooperativity |
| 8 | **Ligand sensor** | sensed_metabolite_id, threshold, response_curve |
| 9 | **Structural fold** | stability, multimerization_n |
| 10 | **Surface tag** | tag_class (pilus_receptor / phage_receptor / surface_antigen) |
| 11 | **Repair/Fidelity** | repair_class (mismatch/proofreading/error-prone), efficiency |

**Cross-cutting parameters**: thermal_stability, pH_optimum, expression_cost ("structural" codons in the block).

**Encoding of continuous parameters**: weighted sum of the values of the codons of the parameter-block (smooth landscape: point mutations → small shifts of the value).

#### Regulatory block — multiple binding sites

The `regulatory_block` of a gene/operon allows **multiple overlappable binding sites** for different σ-factors and for riboswitches → realistic combinatorial regulation (global σ programs + local metabolite-specific regulations).

#### Composition and coherence rules

A protein = sequence of domains. Function emerges from composition and parameters. Examples:

| Function | Composition |
|---|---|
| Glucose transporter | `[Substrate-binding(Glu, Km)] [Channel/pore] [Transmembrane]` |
| Active transporter (ABC-like) | `[Substrate-binding] [Channel/pore] [Energy-coupling] [Transmembrane]` |
| β-lactamase | `[Substrate-binding(β-lactam)] [Catalytic site(hydrolysis)]` |
| Efflux pump | `[Substrate-binding(broad)] [Channel/pore] [Energy-coupling] [Transmembrane]` |
| σ-factor | `[Ligand sensor] [DNA-binding] [Regulator output]` |
| Riboswitch | in `regulatory_block`: `[Ligand sensor] [translation_modulator]` |
| PBP-like | `[Substrate-binding(peptidoglycan_precursor)] [Catalytic site(transpeptidation)] [Transmembrane]` |
| DinB-like (error-prone pol) | `[Substrate-binding(dNTP, low spec)] [Catalytic site(polymerization)] [Repair/Fidelity(error-prone)]` |
| Pilus subunit | `[Structural fold(rigid, n=80)] [Surface tag(pilus_receptor)]` |

**Coherence rules** (emerge from fitness computation, are not hard constraints):
- `Catalytic site` without adjacent `Substrate-binding` → inert
- `Energy-coupling` without `Channel/pore` or `Catalytic` → inert
- `Regulator output` without `DNA-binding` → inert
- `Ligand sensor` isolated → useful only in regulatory_block

"Resistance to a drug" is not a special attribute: it is the result of parameters (high Km on PBP) or the presence of an additional domain (β-lactamase). Consistent with real biology.

#### Mutations and novelty generation

| Mutation type | Effect | Relative frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Point in parameter codon | Continuous drift of the parameter | High (majority) |
| Point in type tag | Categorical flip of the type (discontinuous innovation) | Low (tag = 3 codons) |
| Indel | Boundary shift, tag breakage, frame shift | Medium |
| Duplication | Copy of domain or gene → substrate for neofunctionalization | Medium |
| Inversion | Reverses segment, flips tag orientation | Low |
| **Translocation between genes** | **Fuses domains of different genes → composite chimera** | Low (main engine of true innovation) |

#### Multi-subunit complexes

Declared by `Structural fold(multimerization_n=k)` + `Surface tag` of self-recognition. Activity ∝ min of compatible subunit abundances. No additional abstraction.

#### Biomass balance (no molecular tracking)

Continuous variables per lineage: `membrane_integrity`, `wall_integrity`, `dna_progress` ∈ [0,1].
- Produced by enzymes of the lineage (e.g. PBP-like → wall_integrity), consuming metabolites of Block 6 + ATP
- Degraded by stress (osmotic, antibiotic, lysis)
- `wall_integrity < threshold` during division → lysis → reduction of N or extinction

### Block 8 — Selective pressures

#### A. Continuous pressures (reduce fitness)

1. **Out-of-range physico-chemical stress**: temperature/pH/osmolarity deviate from protein ranges (`thermal_stability`, `pH_optimum`) → progressive drop in kcat (denaturation) → fitness drops.
2. **Metabolite toxicity**: O₂ for obligate anaerobes (induces σ-stress, raises µ via DinB-like); H₂S inhibits cytochromes via competitive binding on Catalytic site of redox; accumulated lactate → acidifies → effect (1).
3. **Metabolic deficiencies**: chronically negative ATP balance → division rate → 0; specific deficiencies (P → no DNA, N → no proteins, Fe → no cofactors, S → no sulfur aa). Selection for low Km in oligotrophic niches.

#### B. Discrete death events

4. **Lysis at division**: probability ∝ `wall_integrity` deficit × osmotic stress.
5. **Phage lysis**: see phage sub-system.
6. **Lethal replication errors**: extreme µ → fraction of non-viable offspring → natural upper bound to µ (emergent error catastrophe).

#### C. Non-selective outflow

7. **Dilution rate**: **per-biotope** parameter (free ecological heterogeneity: ponds vs rivers). Constant fraction of population washed away per tick. Dilutes metabolites, signals, free phages. Coupled inflow of fresh nutrients (classic chemostat). Sets carrying capacity, selects for absolute growth rate.

#### D. Competition

8. **Competition for shared resources**: emergent, no dedicated mechanism. Lineages with low Km or more expressed transporters win the shared pool → others suffer deficiencies.
9. **Bacteriocins**: secreted toxins `[Substrate-binding(target_surface_tag)] [Catalytic(membrane_disruption)]` + "secreted" flag. Target-specific biological warfare between strains → diversification of surfaces for evasion.

#### Phage sub-system

- **Prophage** = cassette in the chromosome/plasmid: receptor (Surface tag), lysogenic repressor (Regulator output + DNA-binding), viral polymerase, capsid (Structural fold × N), lysis genes (Catalytic membrane_disruption).
- **Lysogenic ↔ lytic switch**: stress (DNA damage, σ-SOS) degrades the repressor → induction. Stochastic switch modulated by stress.
- **Free phages**: persistent entities of the biotope, with genome + Surface tag for recognition. Spread via migration (Poisson events — Block 5).
- **Defenses** (covered by existing domains):
  - **Loss-of-receptor**: mutation of the host Surface tag
  - **Restriction-Modification**: enzyme `[Substrate-binding(DNA, palindrome)] [Catalytic(DNA cleavage)]` + methylase with same specificity → arms race on viral palindromes
- **CRISPR**: omitted from v1 (requires qualitatively different adaptive memory). Reserve for future extension.
- **Co-evolution**: phages mutate like Arkeons (same generative system) → genuine arms race.

##### Phase 12 — Implementation status

- **Prophage cassettes** (`Genome.prophage()` → `%{genes, state, repressor_strength}`): the `state :: :lysogenic | :induced` flag makes the commitment to the lytic cycle explicit; `repressor_strength :: float()` (0.0..1.0) modulates sensitivity to stress-driven induction: `p_induction = 0.03 × stress_factor × (1 − repressor_strength)`.
- **Free virions** (`Arkea.Sim.HGT.Virion`): pool of persistent particles per phase, with `genes` (packaged cassette), `surface_signature` (4-codon key for receptor matching), `methylation_profile` (inherited host modification), `decay_age`. Decay via `Phage.decay_step/1` independent of dilution: `decay = 0.20 + 0.05 × decay_age`.
- **`HGT.Phage.lytic_burst/5`**: produces virions into the `phage_pool` of the primary phase (burst size emergent from `Σ multimerization_n` of `:structural_fold` domains in the cassette, biologically plausible range 10–500); releases chromosomal fragments into the `dna_pool` of the same phase (substrate for Phase 13 transformation); removes the cassette from the lysed host's genome.
- **`HGT.Phage.infection_step/4`** (`Tick.step_phage_infection/1` in the pipeline): each virion attempts infection on compatible recipients in the same phase. Receptor matching via `surface_signature`. Uniform gating via `HGT.Defense.restriction_check_virion/3`. Lytic/lysogenic decision driven by the cassette's `repressor_strength`: high repression → lysogeny (child lineage with integrated prophage), low repression → immediate lysis.
- **`HGT.Defense.restriction_check/3`**: generative R-M gate. Site recognition = `signal_key` of the Catalytic site of a gene containing both `:dna_binding` and `:catalytic_site(reaction_class: :hydrolysis)` (restriction) or `:isomerization` (methylation). Bypass via methylation of the donor sharing a `signal_key` with the recipient's enzyme (Arber-Dussoix host-modification). Probabilistic cleavage (`@cleave_p = 0.70` per vulnerable site) to reproduce the in-vivo baseline escape rate (Tock & Dryden 2005).
- **Extended phenotype** (Phase 12): `restriction_profile :: [signal_key]` and `methylation_profile :: [signal_key]` derived from the composition of genes with co-occurring `dna_binding + catalytic_site`.
- **Audit log mobile_elements**: existing schema; the write path for phage-cycle events (`:phage_infection`, `:rm_digestion`) will be wired in Phase 16 with the `HGT.Channel` behaviour.

##### Phase 13 — Natural transformation

- **Free DNA as substrate** (`Arkea.Sim.HGT.DnaFragment`): pool of fragments per phase (`Phase.dna_pool :: %{fragment_id => DnaFragment.t()}`) with `genes` (chromosome of the lysed cell), `methylation_profile` (inherited host modification), `abundance`, `decay_age`, `origin_lineage_id`. Sources: phage lysis (`HGT.Phage.lytic_burst` deposits the chromosome of the lysed cell); future Phases 14+ will add lysis upon division.
- **Emergent competence** (`Phenotype.competence_score :: 0.0..1.0`): non-zero only if the genome expresses the Phase-13 triad — `:channel_pore` (proxy for ComEC/ComEA) + `:transmembrane_anchor` (proxy for type IV pseudopilus) + `:ligand_sensor` (proxy for ComX / induction signal). Score = `min(1.0, geom_mean(n_channel × n_membrane × n_sensor) × 0.2)`. Effective gating threshold 0.10. A naïve genome → competence 0.0 (no free uptake).
- **`HGT.Channel.Transformation.step/4`** (in the `Tick.step_hgt` pipeline, between conjugation and induction): for each competent recipient, iterates over the `dna_pool` of the phase, computes `p_uptake = 0.0006 × competence × fragment.abundance` (cap 0.20), R-M gating via `HGT.Defense.restriction_check/3` with `fragment.methylation_profile`, positional homologous recombination (allelic replacement: gene at position *i* of the donor → position *i* of the recipient if both indices exist).
- **Self-uptake excluded**: `fragment.origin_lineage_id == recipient.id` → rejected (deterministic no-op, prevents inflation).
- **Conservation**: each gate event (digestion, uptake, or homology rejection) consumes one unit of `fragment.abundance`. Fragments at abundance 0 are pruned.
- **Decay**: `Phase.dilute/1` applies the dilution rate to fragments as for free virions; implicit ageing via `decay_age` (prepared for Phase 18 refinement if accelerated decay is needed).
- **Audit log**: existing schema; `:transformation_event` events will be wired in Phase 16 with the `HGT.Channel` behaviour.

##### Phase 14 — Toxicity, elemental constraints, continuous biomass

- **Toxicity** (`Metabolism.toxicity_factor/2` + `Phenotype.detoxify_targets`): three toxic metabolites (`:oxygen`, `:h2s`, `:lactate`) have an encoded `(threshold, scale)`. For each unprotected metabolite, contribution = `1 - max(0, [met] - threshold)/scale`, clamped to `0..1`; contributions compose multiplicatively. Generative detoxify enzyme: a gene with co-occurring `:substrate_binding(target=metabolite)` + `:catalytic_site(reaction_class: :reduction)` → `detoxify_targets ∋ metabolite` → full bypass for that target. Reproduces catalase (O₂), sulfide oxidoreductase (H₂S), lactate dehydrogenase (lactate) as emergent traits.
- **Elemental constraints** (`Metabolism.elemental_factor/3`): for each tracked essential element (P/N/Fe/S) (`@elemental_metabolites`), score = `min(1.0, uptake / floor)` with `floor = 0.001 × abundance`. Geometric mean → global factor. **Only elements that the phenotype actively seeks (`:substrate_binding` present)** count as a constraint; a naïve genome is not penalized for nutrients it does not seek (the prototype does not model cellular recycling). Factor in `0.0..1.0` applied only to biosynthesis (biomass progress), not to respiration.
- **Continuous biomass** (`Lineage.biomass :: %{membrane, wall, dna}`): three components in `0.0..1.0` (default 1.0 = intact founder). Per-tick:
  - `progress`: scaled by `(atp_yield/50) × tox`. Per-component capability: membrane ∝ `n_transmembrane/5`, wall ∝ `n_transmembrane/5` (PBP-like proxy), DNA ∝ `repair_efficiency × elemental`.
  - `decay`: osmotic shock outside the tolerance band ±200 mOsm/L (membrane + wall decay); elemental shortage (DNA decay `(1-elemental) × 0.05`).
- **`Arkea.Sim.Biomass.lysis_probability/1`**: per-component pressure below threshold (membrane 0.30, wall 0.40, DNA 0.25) composes as max → lysis probability in `0..1`.
- **Pipeline** (`Tick`): new `step_biomass` after `step_metabolism` (reuses `state.uptake_by_lineage` to avoid recomputing on an already-drained pool); new `step_lysis` after `step_environment` and before `step_pruning` (Bernoulli trial per lineage, reduces abundance per phase by `floor(count × p)`).
- **State**: `BiotopeState.uptake_by_lineage` added to share uptake from `step_metabolism` to `step_biomass`.
- Phase 17 will use the same biomass as substrate for `error_catastrophe` and SOS coupling.

#### Senescence & error-handling

- **Senescence/aging**: omitted (immortal bacteria at this level of abstraction).

### Block 9 — Quorum sensing & signaling

Cell-cell signaling is a central mechanism of bacterial ecology. Integrated in v1 without new domain types.

#### Signal molecules as fourth environmental class of the biotope

| Class | Role |
|---|---|
| Metabolites (13) | Substrates, acceptors, nutrients, products |
| Xenobiotics | target_class + affinity + mode |
| **Signal molecules** | Cell-cell communication, **no metabolic role** |
| Phage particles | Predation, transductional HGT |

Signals have concentration, decay, dilution like metabolites, but are not consumed by metabolic reactions.

#### Generativity: no catalog

Each signal has a **signature** (4D vector encoded in the parameter codons of the synthase). Mutations of the synthase → slightly different signals (analogy: the different AHLs in the real world).

#### Signaling genes (covered by the existing domains of Block 7)

| Function | Composition |
|---|---|
| Signal synthase | `[Substrate-binding(precursor)] [Catalytic(reaction_class=signal_synthesis, signature=X)]` |
| Signal receptor | `[Ligand sensor(target_signature=Y, threshold)] [DNA-binding] [Regulator output]` |
| Signal transporter | `[Substrate-binding(signature)] [Channel/pore] [Transmembrane]` |

Minimal extensions to the Block 7 taxonomy:
- Addition of `signal_synthesis` to the reaction_class of `Catalytic site`
- Generalization of `sensed_metabolite_id` to `sensed_chemical_id` (metabolites **or** signals)

#### Signal-receptor specificity: continuous matching

```
binding_affinity = exp(− ||signal_signature − receptor_target||² / σ²)
```

Coevolution required between synthase and receptor. Wide `σ` → eavesdropping; narrow `σ` → private communication. Communicative divergence = natural speciation mechanism.

#### Quorum behavior (emergent)

Density-dependence emerges from concentrations:
- Low density → low production → signal below threshold → program OFF
- High density → signal above threshold → program ON

Typical programs under QS control (evolutionary decision or player design): bacteriocins, biofilm formation, conjugation, phage virulence, sporulation-like.

#### Migration, dilution, decay

Signals follow the same rules as metabolites for dilution and migration between biotopes. Spontaneous decay (parameter per signature) by default fast — signals are unstable.

#### Free extensions (emergent from the framework)

- **Inter-species cross-talk** (eavesdropping)
- **Quorum quenching**: signal hydrolases of others' signals `[Substrate-binding(signature)] [Catalytic(hydrolysis)]` → destroys competitors' QS
- **Phage signaling**: phages that release signals upon lysis (call, warning) — emergent if it survives evolutionarily

#### Operational caps

- Maximum distinct coexisting signals per biotope: ~50–100 (with merging if too similar) — tuning parameter
- Signature dimensionality: **4D** (default; supports thousands of dialects)

### Block 10 — Biotope network topology

#### Archetypes (8) and unlock progression

| # | Archetype | Profile | Unlock Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Oxygenated oligotrophic lake | mesophile, neutral pH, low glucose, high O₂, medium dilution | **Tier 1** (starter) |
| 2 | Eutrophic pond | mesophile, neutral/acidic, high glucose, stratified O₂, low dilution | **Tier 1** |
| 3 | Sulfurous marine sediment | anaerobic, high SO₄²⁻, high osmolarity, very low dilution | Tier 2 |
| 4 | Hydrothermal vent | high T (50–80°C), neutral, H₂+H₂S, anaerobic, high dilution | Tier 3 |
| 5 | Acid ferrous drainage | low pH (2–4), high Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, oxic, mesophile | Tier 3 |
| 6 | Methanogenic bog | anaerobic, neutral, rich in organics, cold, very low dilution | Tier 2 |
| 7 | Heterogeneous mesophilic soil | mesophile, neutral, intermittent O₂, variable dilution | **Tier 1** |
| 8 | Saline estuary | mesophile, high osmolarity, nutrient gradient, high dilution | Tier 2 |

Intra-type heterogeneity: base parameters are sampled around the archetype centroid. Precise unlock criteria for Tier 2/3 are balancing parameters (evolutionary/temporal milestones).

#### Geometric topology

- **2D abstract planar** graph (navigable map)
- Each biotope: coordinates `(x, y)` + 3–5 nearest neighbors
- **Long-range bridges**: ~1–2% of arcs → inter-regional HGT, connected world
- **Clustered geographical distribution**: archetypes form *zones* (hydrothermal region, marshy region, marine region, etc.). At zone borders intermediate biotopes emerge → natural ecological gradients. Bridges connect distant zones rarely.

#### Arcs: weights and directionality

- `edge_weight` ∝ 1/geographical distance
- `biotope_compatibility(A, B)` derived from distance in the environmental profile (T, pH, osmolarity) → a thermophile does not establish in the cold lake
- **Per-archetype directionality**: symmetric for ponds (lakes, bogs), asymmetric for flowing (springs=upstream, sediments=downstream)
- Along the arcs flow (with independent scaling): lineages, free phages, metabolites, signals

#### Allocation and colonization (multi-biotope, cap 3)

Cap **3 biotopes per player** = 1 home + up to 2 colonized.

- New player: chooses Tier 1 archetype → home biotope in the corresponding zone
- Tier 2 and Tier 3 unlocked by progression
- **Colonization mechanism** (relying on migration of Block 5 + lineages of Block 4):
  1. Player's lineages migrate into neighboring biotopes
  2. If a lineage originated in the home reaches **abundance ≥ threshold** in a **wild adjacent** biotope, maintained for **≥ N ticks**, the biotope is **claimed**
  3. **First-to-threshold** in case of competition between players
  4. Only wild biotopes are colonizable (no claim on others' biotopes — consistent with no-direct-PvP)
- **Emergent expansion from evolution**: specialists colonize their own zone easily, generalists more widely but with less specialization. For distant zones: cross-tolerance or fortunate bridge edge.

#### Abandoned biotopes → wild *(default to be confirmed)*

Player inactive for ≥ T days → biotopes return to **wild**:
- Population persists and continues to evolve autonomously
- Intervention rights revoked
- Biotope colonizable again (also by the original player if they return)

**Simultaneously resolves**: Arkeon offline-state behavior (Block 1) + churn/saturation of the network (wilds are colonization reserves for newcomers).

#### World sizing

| Resource | Steady-state quantity |
|---|---|
| Fixed natural biotopes (at launch) | ~200–400 distributed over the 8 archetypes and zones |
| Player home biotopes | 1 per active player |
| Player colonized biotopes | 0–2 per active player |
| Wild biotopes (ex-inactive players) | growing over time, colonization reserve |

**Simulation cost (estimate)**: cap 1,000 lineages/biotope × ~10⁴ total biotopes at steady state = 10⁷ lineage updates per tick. With tick every 5 min (see Block 11), well within the reach of a moderate cluster.

### Block 11 — Temporal sizing

#### Fundamental unit

- **1 tick = 5 real minutes = 1 reference generation** (ideal Arkeon under perfect conditions)
- Real lineages vary: stressed ones may require 5–10 ticks to divide; in bloom 1 tick. The "reference generation" is just a unit of kinetic calibration.
- "Slow game" choice: privileges evolutionary depth and multi-session gameplay over fast feedback.

#### Scale of evolutionary phenomena

| Real time | Tick / generations | Observable by player |
|---|---|---|
| 5 min | 1 | Isolated mutations; response to acute stress |
| 30 min | 6 | Visible drift; first variants |
| 1 hour | 12 | Visible directional selection; effects of first interventions |
| Session 2h | 24 | Consolidated selective events |
| Session 5h | 60 | Partial use case (visible resistances) |
| 1 day | ~288 | Complete resistance; phage-host co-evolution started |
| 1 week | ~2,000 | Complete co-evolution; colonization; initial speciation |
| 1 month | ~8,600 | Macro-evolution: change of dominant archetype in zone; communicative speciation |

The "antibiotic resistance in 100 generations" use case of Block 5 requires **~8 real hours** → multi-session, consistent with persistent MMO.

#### Tick kinetics (execution order)

For each biotope, per tick:
1. Metabolic balance (steady-state proto-FBA on intracellular pools + environment)
2. Gene expression (σ-factor, riboswitch, regulators evaluated; levels updated)
3. Cellular events (divisions, deaths, lysis, mutations → new lineages)
4. HGT (conjugation/transformation/transduction between lineages of the biotope)
5. Environmental events (metabolite/signal decay, dilution)
6. Pruning (lineages below threshold → locally extinct, preserved in the global tree)

Between ticks, global coordinated step:

7. Inter-biotope migration (flows on arcs: lineages, phages, metabolites, signals)

#### Server: walltime = simulation clock

- Server processes **exactly 1 tick every 5 minutes wall-clock**
- **Always uniform time** — no local fast-forward (consistent with unitary MMO)
- All players share the same "now"
- Server restart: tick stays synchronized with wall-clock; on restart, resumes from the tick corresponding to the current time

**Parallelization**: biotopes are independent during steps 1–6 (intra-tick) → massively parallelizable. Step 7 requires synchronization between neighboring arcs → globally coordinated step.

#### Persistence

- **Full DB snapshot every 10 ticks (50 real minutes)**: state of all biotopes
- **Write-ahead log** intra-snapshot for every change (new lineage, HGT, mutation, player intervention) → complete recovery in case of crash
- **Historical phylogenetic tree** written incrementally with periodic decimation (Block 4)

#### Client-side polling

- Overview view: server polling every 15–30 seconds
- Zoom view on biotope: polling 5–10 seconds
- Notable events (dominant extinctions, colonizations, QS peaks, phage mass lysis) → asynchronous push notifications

#### Computational cost (revised with 5-min tick)

- 10⁴ biotopes × 1,000 lineages cap = 10⁷ lineage updates per tick
- 1 tick = 300 sec of available walltime → ~33k updates/sec required
- At ~1 µs per lineage update (optimistic) = 10s of CPU per tick → ample margin
- With realism 10× worse, still 100s out of 300s → **3× margin**
- At steady state with 10⁴ biotopes → **2–5 node cluster** sufficient with shard per region

#### Perceptual latency of interventions

Player actions (antibiotic, nutrients, inoculum, plasmid) → applied at the next tick. **Maximum latency 5 minutes**. Effects measurable in 5–30 ticks (25–150 minutes). Cadence consistent with "slow" strategic MMO.

### Block 12 — Micro/macroscale boundary

#### Phase-based model

Each biotope is subdivided into **2–3 phases** (conceptual sub-environments, not spatially discretized). The simulation is authoritative **at the phase level**, not at the 2D position level.

#### Number of phases per archetype (variable)

| Archetype | Phases (authoritative) |
|---|---|
| Oligotrophic lake | `surface (oxic)`, `water_column (semi-mixed)` |
| Eutrophic pond | `surface (oxic)`, `water_column`, `sediment (anoxic)` |
| Marine sediment | `interface (gradient)`, `bulk_sediment (anoxic)` |
| Hydrothermal vent | `vent_core (hot)`, `mixing_zone (gradient T)` |
| Acid drainage | `acid_water`, `mineral_surface (biofilm-friendly)` |
| Methanogenic bog | `surface_oxic`, `peat_core (anoxic)` |
| Heterogeneous soil | `aerated_pore`, `wet_clump (anoxic)`, `soil_water` |
| Saline estuary | `freshwater_layer`, `mixing_zone`, `marine_layer` |

Each phase has:
- Its own pools of metabolites, signals, free phages
- Its own subsets of lineages (each lineage has abundance per phase, possibly zero in some)
- Its own environmental parameters (T, pH, osmolarity) — may diverge from biotope-average
- Its own dilution rate (e.g. surface high, sediment very low)

#### What is authoritative vs visualization

| Aspect | Authoritative | Pure visualization |
|---|---|---|
| Lineage abundances per phase | ✅ | — |
| Metabolite / signal / phage concentrations per phase | ✅ | — |
| Environmental parameters per phase | ✅ | — |
| 2D individual positions of "dots" | — | ✅ derived from phase distribution |
| Biofilm shapes, colonies, animations | — | ✅ procedural from phase density |
| Interpolated concentration heatmap | — | ✅ from phase concentration |
| Phage lysis waves | — | ✅ animation of mass lysis event |

#### Phase preference: encoded in the genome

Covered by the domains of Block 7, without new types:
- **Adhesion → biofilm/surface**: `Surface tag(adhesin)` + `Structural fold(rigid)`
- **Motility → water_column**: flagellar subunits (`Structural fold(filamentous)` + `Energy-coupling`)
- **O₂ tolerance → oxic phases**: presence of protective genes (catalase-like)
- **Physical stress tolerance → extreme phases**: thermal_stability/pH_optimum of proteins

Distribution between phases computed per tick as a function of lineage phenotype and phase parameters. Emergent, not player choice.

#### Phase-to-phase migration

Migration (Block 5) flows **at the phase level**: a cell in `surface` of A migrates preferentially toward `surface` of B. `biotope_compatibility` generalized to `phase_compatibility(A.phase_x, B.phase_y)`.

Consequence: a "biofilm-ist" lineage moves little (anchored), a "planktonic" one diffuses rapidly. Consistent with real biology.

#### Player interventions

Granularity: **biotope-wide or phase-level**, never precise 2D coordinate.

Examples:
- "Antibiotic in the sediment of my pond" — phase-level
- "Inoculum plasmid in surface" — phase-level
- "Mixing event" — **available strategic action**: temporarily remixes the phases (homogenizes concentrations and populations), with metabolic/resource cost for the player. Allows maneuvers like "I expose the anaerobes to O₂" or "I dilute a toxic metabolite throughout the volume"
- "Click a specific dot in the 2D view" — **not available** (position is not authoritative)

#### 2D visualization: procedural rendering

- Phase abundances determine screen regions (e.g. upper band = surface, lower band = sediment)
- The "dots" represent fractions of lineages, randomly positioned within the region of their phase
- Rendering density ∝ N of the lineage (with procedural clustering to simulate colonies/biofilms)
- Coloring by phenotypic cluster (Block 4)
- Events (mass lysis, selective sweep) as procedural animated effects above the base rendering

The rendering is **derived** from the authoritative state, does not influence it.

#### Additional cost

10⁴ biotopes × 2.5 average phases × 1k lineages = ~2.5 × 10⁷ lineage-phase updates per tick. With 300s walltime, ample margin (≥ 3×) relative to the budget of Block 11.

### Block 13 — Residual anti-griefing

#### Identified vectors

Despite the no-direct-PvP choice (Block 3), indirect ways remain to harm other players:

| # | Vector | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Plasmid burden flooding | Costly/non-useful plasmid released → migration + HGT → weakens recipients |
| 2 | Hyper-virulent phage release | Hyper-aggressive phage → free particles migrate (Poisson) → infect others |
| 3 | Bacteriocin shotgun | Bacteriocins evolved for common surface tags → spread via migration |
| 4 | Signal spam / quorum quenching | Signal flooding or anti-signal hydrolases → disrupts others' QS |
| 5 | Resource depletion in colonized wild | Hyperconsumption of metabolites → deficiency for others' migrants |
| 6 | Colonization grabbing | Rapid colonization of adjacent wilds to wall in |
| 7 | Abandoned-biotope seeding | "Trojan" pre-evolution + abandonment → permanent harmful wild |
| 8 | Multi-account coordination | Multiple coordinated accounts to amplify attacks |

#### Mitigations already covered by the design

Most vectors are self-neutralized by previous blocks:

- **Dilution + decay** (Blocks 3, 8, 9, 10): all effects decay with distance from the release point. Attacks on players in distant zones impossible.
- **Plasmid cost** (Block 5): burden plasmid selected against → extinct in few generations if not advantageous. **Vector #1 self-neutralized**.
- **Phage-host co-evolution** (Block 8): RM and loss-of-receptor defenses evolve naturally. No sustainable "invincible phage". **Vector #2 exhausts itself**.
- **Target-match bacteriocins** (Block 8): effective only against specific Surface tags → diversification of surfaces (Block 7) makes them ineffective at scale. **Vector #3 limited**.
- **Real time as bottleneck** (Block 11): all interventions require wall-clock. High human cost of griefing.
- **Colonization cap 2** (Block 10): walling-in scale limited.
- **Distance in the network** (Block 10): clustered zones + rare bridge edges → cross-zone attacks must traverse intermediate biotopes.

#### Explicit residual mitigations

**A. Cooldown on colonization**
After a successful colonization: **24h real cooldown** before the next one. Limits "grabbing" without penalizing organic expansion (1–2 colonizations/week is reasonable scope). *Mitigates vector #6.*

**B. Audit log of mobile elements (origin tracking)**
Each mobile element (plasmid, prophage) carries an in-game-invisible metadata with `origin_lineage_id` and `origin_biotope_id`. Allows ops systems to detect abusive patterns (e.g. "phage X originated by player Y appears in N biotopes attached to player Z within 1 week"). *Mitigates vectors #1, #2, #7.*

**C. Claim sustainability**
Beyond the abundance threshold (Block 10): the claim holds only if the lineage's population grows or remains stable for **N ticks post-claim**. If it collapses → claim revoked, biotope returns to wild. Penalizes opportunistic grabs, rewards real adaptation. *Mitigates vector #6.*

**D. Intervention budget per biotope**
Regenerative cap on player interventions: e.g. **1 major intervention every 30 real minutes per biotope**. Not punitive in normal play (interventions are sparingly used anyway), shuts down automated abuse. *Mitigates vectors #1, #2, #4 in flooding mode.*

#### "No action needed" documented

**E. Pre-evolution + abandonment (vector #7)**: wilds follow normal ecology. Natural selection erodes purely harmful traits without benefit to the carrier (penalizes them by metabolic/genomic cost). **Structural mitigation**: the ecological design prevents the persistence of "harmful-without-benefit" traits. No explicit action necessary.

**F. Multi-account / bot (vector #8)**: **operational** topic (registration, IP detection, behavioral analysis), not of ecological design. Must be addressed in the operational plan but out of scope for the biological model.

### Block 14 — Technological stack

#### Runtime decision: BEAM/Elixir

**Sim core + orchestration**: Elixir + OTP. The strengths of BEAM align with Arkea's needs:
- Fault tolerance via supervision trees ("let it crash + recover") for 24/7 sim
- Distributed cluster out-of-the-box via `libcluster` + `:net_kernel`
- Lightweight process model (~3 KB/process): one GenServer per biotope is natural and scales
- Phoenix Channels + Presence + PubSub for real-time push to client
- Hot code reload for deploy without downtime on persistent MMO

**Known weakness**: pure numerical performance (10⁻100× slower than Rust). **Future mitigation**: extract the hot kernels (proto-FBA, parameter computation from codons, batch kinetic update) as **NIF Rust** via Rustler/Zigler when profiling justifies it. **For the prototype: pure Elixir**, optimize only after profiling real bottlenecks.

#### Complete stack

| Layer | Choice |
|---|---|
| Sim core + orchestration | **Elixir + OTP** |
| Web framework | **Phoenix** |
| Server-rendered UI (chrome, dashboard, controls, phylogenetic view) | **Phoenix LiveView** |
| Real-time data to client | **Phoenix Channels + PubSub + Presence** |
| 2D WebGL in the browser | **TypeScript + PixiJS** (mounted in a LiveView Hook) |
| Hook ↔ LiveView coordination | `phx-hook`, `pushEvent`, server push events |
| DB | **PostgreSQL** via Ecto |
| Numeric hot path (deferred) | NIF Rust via Rustler |
| Multi-node cluster (deferred) | libcluster + Horde/Swarm |
| Background jobs (snapshot, decimation) | Oban |
| Telemetry | `:telemetry` + LiveDashboard → Prometheus exporter (production) |

### Block 15 — Integral use case (tabletop stress test)

Walk-through of a multi-week campaign that traverses all 14 previous blocks in a continuous scenario. Purpose: validate internal coherence before implementation.

#### Setup

- **Anna** (Tier 1 account): home `STAGNO-A` (Eutrophic pond archetype) in marshy zone. Gram-positive Arkeon, thick wall, facultative aerobic heterotroph + fermenter.
- **Bartolomeo** (Tier 2 account): home `ESTUARIO-B` (Saline estuary archetype) in coastal zone. Halophilic Gram-negative Arkeon.
- **Topology**: zones connected by a rare bridge edge (~5 graph hops away).
- **Time**: 30 real days ≈ 8,640 ticks ≈ 8,600 generations.

#### Week 1 — Settlement and emergent biofilm

- Anna inoculates 10 cells in STAGNO-A; exponential growth to 10⁴; emergent phase distribution (70% water_column / 25% surface / 5% sediment)
- Point drift produces 30–40 daughter lineages; mild selection; active pruning
- Anna feeds extra-glucose in `surface` (intervention budget −1) → QS threshold activated → emergent biofilm in surface; distribution shifts to 50/35/15

[Blocks: 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13]

#### Week 2 — Stress, mutator, resistance

- Anna introduces β-lactam-like (0.5× MIC) in water_column; mass lysis 10⁶ → 10⁴; surface biofilm and sediment protected
- σ-stress activates DinB-like (µ × 50); point mutation on PBP-like shifts Km → partial resistance fixed
- Dose raised to 1× MIC; duplication of an efflux transporter + neofunctionalization → MIC 4×; removed, balanced polymorphism by cost

[Blocks: 4, 5, 7, 8, 12]

#### Week 3 — Prophage, defenses, first colonization

- Free phage arrives from wild (Poisson); direct lysis + lysogeny
- Defenses evolve: loss-of-receptor (with cost) + Restriction-Modification (DNA hydrolase + methylase tandem from translocation)
- Anna colonizes LAGO-W (planktonic sub-lineage reaches 30% for >100 ticks → claim, 24h cooldown triggers)
- Anna induces communicative separation in LAGO-W: synthase drifts → signal outside σ of the receptor of STAGNO-A → partial communicative speciation

[Blocks: 3, 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, 13]

#### Week 4 — The indirect attack

- Bartolomeo releases a non-conjugative burden plasmid from ESTUARIO-B (audit log: origin_lineage_id, origin_biotope_id)
- Plasmid cost → local spread stabilizes at ~5%
- Migration along bridge edge: dilution + selection through 5 intermediate hops → fraction < 0.1% on arrival in Anna's zone
- In LAGO-W only passive transformation (non-conjugative) → < 0.05%
- Selection against completes elimination in 500 generations; no damage to Anna; principle E ("no action needed") confirmed

[Blocks: 5, 8, 10, 13]

#### Week 5 — Macro-evolution

- STAGNO-A: dominant founder lineage; forest at 2,000 lineages (cap 1,000 + pruning, decimation of historical tree)
- LAGO-W: chimera by translocation fuses `Substrate-binding(Fe³⁺)` + `Catalytic(reduction)` → new ferric reductase → niche in sub-oxic phases; **composite innovation**
- The two strains genetically and communicatively distinct, HGT-linked by migration

[Blocks: 4, 7, 9]

| 2. Biological model | ✅ operons, σ-factor, riboswitch, rearrangements |

**Partial coverage to reinforce in future use cases** (not design gaps):
- Chemolithotrophy (hydrogenotrophic thermophile in hydrothermal vent → H₂/H₂S/CH₄)
- Toxicity of accumulated natural metabolites (H₂S, lactate)
- Bacteriocins
- Long-term phage-host co-evolution (10⁴+ generations)

#### Design gaps emerged

Unlike the first use case (8 gaps), the integral walk-through **does not reveal serious inconsistencies**. Three minor considerations, of balancing/implementation:

1. **Intervention budget vs exploratory sessions**: 1 action/30 min may be tight for those who experiment intensively. Possible accumulable pool up to N stored actions. Balancing decision.
2. **Phase distribution for newborns**: a just-born lineage inherits the parent's distribution (snapshot at the instant of birth), recomputes on the next tick. To be clarified in implementation.
3. **Cap 1,000 in just-colonized wild**: resident wild lineages stay and follow the same rules. Wildness = flag of non-control, no different mechanical treatment. To be confirmed in implementation.

#### Conclusion

**Internally coherent** design. The 14 blocks integrate into a continuous scenario that produces biologically plausible phenomena: resistance, biofilm, prophages, RM, colonization, speciation, composite innovation, failure of an indirect attack. The 3 remaining points are refinements, not gaps. **Ready for the implementation phase.**
