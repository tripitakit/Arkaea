# Arkea вАФ Inception & Design Document

## Context

**Arkea** is a game/simulation of evolution of proto-bacterial organisms (the *Arkeons*). The project's goal is to create an experience that is:

- **Scientifically accurate**, addressed to an audience of biologists, microbiologists, geneticists and molecular biologists
- A **web app with 2D WebGL UI**
- Centered on the creation of an Arkeon: the player defines its **cellular structure** (membrane, wall, integrated proteins) and **genome** (structural and enzymatic genes for metabolism and replication)
- Capable of simulating **mobile genetic elements** (plasmids, prophages) for the horizontal transfer of traits, metabolic pathways and resistances

The guiding constraint is: **simplify by several orders of magnitude** the complexity of a real bacterial genome, **without trivializing the biology**. The expert audience must recognize real mechanisms (operons, ѕГ-factor regulation, riboswitch, integrases, point mutagenesis, rearrangements) and be able to reason with their own expertise.

This document consolidates the scope decisions taken in the first brainstorming sessions and remains the design reference from which to derive subsequent implementation plans.

## Consolidated decisions

### Block 1 вАФ World architecture

| Axis | Choice |
|---|---|
| Genre | Evolutionary sandbox + strategic-competitive ecosystem (hybrid) |
| Persistence | Persistent world, 24/7 server-side simulation |
| Multiplayer | **Single shared world (MMO-like)**: all players populate the same ecosystem |
| Time | **Continuous accelerated even offline**: the ecosystem evolves regardless of player presence |
| Player role | **Designer + Breeder/engineer**: designs the initial Arkeon and then actively intervenes (selection, plasmid introduction, targeted genomic modifications) |

**Known tensions deferred to a later phase:**
- Behavior of the player's Arkeon when offline (possible extinction? stasis? AI autopilot?)
- Onboarding of new players in an already evolved world
- Anti-griefing and competitive balancing
- Infrastructural costs of the 24/7 simulation
- Sharding/scaling as the player population grows

### Block 2 вАФ Granularity of the biological model

**Choice: "B+C" level вАФ modular with sub-genic touches.**

#### Genome
- **Chromosome** = ordered sequence of **genes** (discrete objects) grouped in **operons**
- Each gene has:
  - **Functional identity** generated by an **abstract/generative system** (functions emerge from composable parameters, not from a finite predefined catalog)
  - **Quantitative parameters** (Km, Vmax, affinity, stability) вАФ substrate of selection
  - **Short symbolic sequence** (~50-200 logical codons, not real ATGC) sufficient to support point mutations, recombination hotspots, integration sites
- **Plasmids and prophages** = separate genomic fragments with their own dynamics (replication, loss, chromosomal integration, excision)

#### Gene regulation (chosen depth)
- **Operons** with on/off promoters modulated by **environmental metabolites** (induction/repression)
- **Sigma factor** for global transcriptional programs (e.g. stress response, sporulation-like, stationary phase)
- **Riboswitch** for fast post-transcriptional regulation (e.g. direct binding to metabolite)

#### Supported mutations
- Point (logical codon substitutions)
- Indels
- Gene duplications (substrate for neofunctionalization)
- **Large rearrangements**: inversions and translocations

#### Metabolism
- 8-15 key metabolites as "chemical currency" (exact set to be defined in Block 3)
- Reactions catalyzed by enzymes encoded in the genome, simplified Michaelis-Menten kinetics
- ATP-equivalent balance as a central constraint (replication, protein synthesis, active transport cost)
- Steady-state proto-FBA recomputed per tick (not molecular simulation)

#### Membrane and wall
- **Membrane**: lipid composition (fluidity, permeability) + integral proteins (porins, transporters, receptors, efflux pumps)
- **Wall**: type (absent / Gram-like thin / Gram-like thick / S-layer) вЖТ metabolic cost + resistance to osmotic stress, lysis, phages
- It is the main **visible phenotype** on the 2D WebGL UI

#### Deliberate exclusions
- No real ribosomes/translation (genes produce enzymes at a parametric rate)
- No real replisome (division = event with cost + error rate)
- No complex intracellular structures (no compartments, no cytoskeleton)
- No real ATGC DNA (more abstract symbolic sequence)

**Rationale of the generative system for functions**: a finite catalog (e.g. 200 predefined KEGG families) would be more readable but would close the evolutionary space. A generative system (composable parameters that produce new functions under selective pressure) preserves the open nature of evolution, at the cost of greater complexity in modeling.

### Block 3 вАФ Environment and selective pressure

#### Spatiality вАФ hybrid two-scale model

**Macroscale**: the world is a graph of **multiple connected biotopes**. Each biotope is a local chemostat with its own chemistry (metabolite concentrations), physical parameters (temperature, pH, osmolarity), and Arkeon population. **Migration** between biotopes happens via parametric flows/dispersion. This is the scale on which the 24/7 server-side simulation runs.

**Microscale**: when a player observes a biotope up close, a **local continuous 2D representation** of the same population and chemistry is presented. The microscale is predominantly for **visualization/interaction** (detailed physics is not authoritative for the global simulation, but the player sees gradients, biofilms, colonies). This allows for rich WebGL UI without paying the cost of 2D diffusion everywhere.

**Consequences:**
- Ecological niches are global (per biotope) but visually recognizable
- The simulation cost scales with the number of biotopes, not with the visualized area
- The design must clarify what of the microscale is authoritative (e.g. local player events?) and what is purely decorative

#### Events and perturbations вАФ system + player on their own biotope

- **Systemic events**: seasonal cycles, random perturbations, natural evolution of phages and other mobile elements, nutrient arrivals
- **Player interventions**: limited to the **own biotope** вАФ can introduce antibiotics, regulate nutrients, inoculate strains, release plasmids, modify physical parameters
- **No direct PvP**: players cannot perturb others' biotopes. Competition between players occurs **indirectly** via:
  - Migration of Arkeons and mobile elements between connected biotopes
  - Evolutionary success of one's own strains in the network's biotopes
  - Possible ecological "leaderboard" (niche occupation, dominance, diversity)

This choice drastically reduces anti-griefing requirements and keeps the game accessible, while preserving genuine ecological interaction via inter-biotope flows.

#### Sub-themes still to be defined (deferred to dedicated exercises)

1. **Chemical inventory**: 8-15 key metabolites (glucose, NHвВГ, OвВВ, lactate, HвВВS, POвВД, Fe¬≤вБЇ...). To be anchored to real microbiology.
2. **Selective pressures**: mechanisms of death and loss (osmotic lysis, starvation, phage predation, antibiosis, competition, dilution).
3. **Network topology of biotopes**: number, connections, flow rules, environmental heterogeneity.
4. **What of the microscale is authoritative** vs purely visualization.

### Block 4 вАФ Population model

**Choice: polymorphic population with per-lineage tracking (lineage-based modeling).**

The base unit is the **lineage** (a distinct genotype + an abundance), not the cell. Each biotope maintains a **lineage forest** each one with complete genome, abundance NбµҐ, fitness wбµҐ, pointer to parent вЖТ reconstructable phylogenetic tree.

#### Tick dynamics (per biotope)

1. **Differential growth** of the lineage dependent on fitness √Ч environment вИТ metabolic costs вИТ death rate вИТ dilution
2. **Mutation**: probability ¬µ at each division (modulated by stress ѕГ-factor) вЖТ a new daughter lineage is born
3. **HGT**: probabilistic events of conjugation/transduction/transformation between lineages of the biotope (including migrants) вЖТ new lineage with acquired mobile element
4. **Migration**: fraction of abundance flows to neighboring biotopes maintaining the genotype
5. **Pruning**: lineages with N below threshold removed from the local forest (but preserved in the global phylogenetic tree)

#### Genomic storage вАФ delta encoding

- Reference genome for each founder strain
- Each lineage stores only a **delta** (list of mutations relative to the reference of its own clade), like VCF
- Large rearrangements may consolidate a new reference if the delta becomes excessive

#### Operational caps (decided 2026-04-25)

- **Lineages cap per biotope**: **1,000**
- **Policy at cap reach**: **extinction of the rarest lineages** (the least abundant are eliminated from the local forest)
- **Historical phylogenetic tree**: **periodic decimation** (keep only ramifications of interest, e.g. those that reached a historical maximum abundance above threshold)

#### Implications on UI and DB

- **UI**: aggregation by phenotypic cluster (1,000 lineages are not individually readable); optional phylogenetic view; highlight of player's lineages and dominant ones
- **DB**: table `lineages(id, biotope_id, parent_id, abundance, delta_genome, fitness_cache)`; historical tree in separate table, periodically decimated

### Block 5 вАФ Mechanisms of the biological engine

**Unifying principle**: *everything is metabolism* (including drugs) and *everything is encoded in the genome* (including conjugation, mutation rate, costs). No special mechanism "above" the biological model.

#### Xenobiotics as special metabolites (target вЖТ drug вЖТ effect)

Each drug/xenobiotic is a metabolite of the chemical model, with concentration in the biotope, transport via encoded porins/efflux, and possible enzymatic degradation (e.g. ќ≤-lactamase вЖТ hydrolysis). In addition it has:
- **`target_class`** (e.g. `pbp_like`, `ribosome_like`, `dna_polymerase_like`, `membrane`, `efflux_pump`)
- **`affinity`** (Kd toward the target)
- **`mode`** (cidal vs static; mechanism: blocks-synthesis, perforates-membrane, induces-mismutationsвА¶)

When present, it binds the target proteins of the lineage with rate вИЭ `[drug] √Ч [target] / Kd`. The bound target is non-functional вЖТ consequence dependent on the target. The "MIC" emerges from the amount of free target the lineage maintains.

**Consequence**: no separate "antibiotics" system. A single framework covers ќ≤-lactams, aminoglycoside-likes, fluoroquinolone-likes, etc., and resistance evolution emerges from the same mechanisms that handle every metabolism.

#### Modulable mutation rate (SOS-like)

Each lineage has a current ¬µ:

```
¬µ = ¬µ_baseline √Ч ќ† (1 + ќ±_i √Ч expr(repair_gene_i))
```

where the `repair_gene_i` are encoded repair/fidelity genes (`mutS_like`, `dnaQ_like`, etc.). In addition, stress ѕГ-factor can activate an **error-prone polymerase** (DinB/Pol-V-like) that raises ¬µ directly. **The mutation rate is an evolvable phenotype** вАФ mutator strains emerge naturally.

#### Gene-encoded conjugation

A plasmid is conjugative if and only if it carries `pili_like + relaxase_like + oriT_like`. Conjugation rate between donor D and recipient R in the same biotope:

```
rate = expr(pili_D) √Ч NбіЕ √Ч N А √Ч compatibility(D, R)
```

`compatibility` depends on the recipient's surface receptors, entry-exclusion, etc. Non-conjugative plasmids move by transformation (free DNA) or mobilization through helper plasmid.

#### Plasmid cost

Two components, no "slots" (the "slot cost" is an artifact of representation, not biology):
- **Replication**: ATP proportional to plasmid size (number of genes √Ч cost per gene), paid at each division
- **Transcriptional burden**: each tick, sum of expression of plasmid genes √Ч cost per protein (amino-acid-eq / ATP)

Large and strongly expressed plasmids are costly вЖТ natural trade-off: useful under selective pressure, lost by dilution when pressure ceases.

#### Migration between biotopes

**Continuous drip as default**. For each arc A вЖТ B and each lineage i:

```
ќФNбµҐ_migrated = base_flow √Ч edge_weight √Ч (NбµҐ_A / N_total_A) √Ч biotope_compatibility
```

`base_flow` global; `edge_weight` reflects connectivity (narrow channel vs broad flow); `biotope_compatibility` reduces migration between very different environments.

**Discrete events (Poisson)** reserved for rare phenomena: free phage transport, mass dispersion (airborne), catastrophic events.

#### Generative system for gene functions вАФ discontinuous innovations

Gene functions emerge from a system **with domain composition**, not from a finite catalog nor from continuous parameters alone:

1. **Mixed discrete+continuous function space**: each protein has a descriptor `(categorical_functional_domain, continuous_parameters)`. Point mutations typically change continuous parameters (fine tuning). Mutations in "structural" codons can **flip the categorical_functional_domain** вАФ rare but possible events (e.g. change of substrate specificity in a transporter).
2. **Fusion/scission via large rearrangements**: a translocation that fuses two genes produces a chimeric protein with genuinely new function (combination of domains). It is the main mechanism of real evolutionary innovation, and the model explicitly supports it.

**Consequence**: the model allows three regimes of evolution: parametric drift (tuning), specificity jumps (domain change), composite innovation (fusion). All emerge from the same mutation system.

### Block 7 вАФ Generative system of functional domains

#### Gene structure

A gene is an ordered sequence of **logical codons** (50вАУ200 symbols), over an alphabet of **20 symbols** (analogous to amino acids). Codons group into consecutive blocks, each of which encodes a **functional domain**:

```
gene = [ promoter_block ] [ regulatory_block (optional) ] domain_1 domain_2 ... domain_N
```

Each domain:
- **Type tag** (first ~3 codons): categorically identifies the type
- **Parameter codons** (rest, 10вАУ30 codons): encode the continuous parameters

Typical length: 2вАУ10 domains per gene, consistent with real proteins.

#### Domain type taxonomy (11)

| # | Type | Key continuous parameters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Substrate-binding pocket** | target_metabolite_id, Km, specificity_breadth |
| 2 | **Catalytic site** | reaction_class (hyd/ox/red/isom/lig/lyase), kcat, cofactor |
| 3 | **Transmembrane anchor** | hydrophobicity, n_passes |
| 4 | **Channel/pore** | selectivity, gating_threshold |
| 5 | **Energy-coupling site** | atp_cost, pmf_coupling |
| 6 | **DNA-binding** | promoter_specificity, affinity |
| 7 | **Regulator output** | mode (activator/repressor), cooperativity |
| 8 | **Ligand sensor** | sensed_metabolite_id, threshold, response_curve |
| 9 | **Structural fold** | stability, multimerization_n |
| 10 | **Surface tag** | tag_class (pilus_receptor / phage_receptor / surface_antigen) |
| 11 | **Repair/Fidelity** | repair_class (mismatch/proofreading/error-prone), efficiency |

**Cross-cutting parameters**: thermal_stability, pH_optimum, expression_cost ("structural" codons in the block).

**Encoding of continuous parameters**: weighted sum of the values of the codons of the parameter-block (smooth landscape: point mutations вЖТ small shifts of the value).

#### Regulatory block вАФ multiple binding sites

The `regulatory_block` of a gene/operon allows **multiple overlappable binding sites** for different ѕГ-factors and for riboswitches вЖТ realistic combinatorial regulation (global ѕГ programs + local metabolite-specific regulations).

#### Composition and coherence rules

A protein = sequence of domains. Function emerges from composition and parameters. Examples:

| Function | Composition |
|---|---|
| Glucose transporter | `[Substrate-binding(Glu, Km)] [Channel/pore] [Transmembrane]` |
| Active transporter (ABC-like) | `[Substrate-binding] [Channel/pore] [Energy-coupling] [Transmembrane]` |
| ќ≤-lactamase | `[Substrate-binding(ќ≤-lactam)] [Catalytic site(hydrolysis)]` |
| Efflux pump | `[Substrate-binding(broad)] [Channel/pore] [Energy-coupling] [Transmembrane]` |
| ѕГ-factor | `[Ligand sensor] [DNA-binding] [Regulator output]` |
| Riboswitch | in `regulatory_block`: `[Ligand sensor] [translation_modulator]` |
| PBP-like | `[Substrate-binding(peptidoglycan_precursor)] [Catalytic site(transpeptidation)] [Transmembrane]` |
| DinB-like (error-prone pol) | `[Substrate-binding(dNTP, low spec)] [Catalytic site(polymerization)] [Repair/Fidelity(error-prone)]` |
| Pilus subunit | `[Structural fold(rigid, n=80)] [Surface tag(pilus_receptor)]` |

**Coherence rules** (emerge from fitness computation, are not hard constraints):
- `Catalytic site` without adjacent `Substrate-binding` вЖТ inert
- `Energy-coupling` without `Channel/pore` or `Catalytic` вЖТ inert
- `Regulator output` without `DNA-binding` вЖТ inert
- `Ligand sensor` isolated вЖТ useful only in regulatory_block

"Resistance to a drug" is not a special attribute: it is the result of parameters (high Km on PBP) or the presence of an additional domain (ќ≤-lactamase). Consistent with real biology.

#### Mutations and novelty generation

| Mutation type | Effect | Relative frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Point in parameter codon | Continuous drift of the parameter | High (majority) |
| Point in type tag | Categorical flip of the type (discontinuous innovation) | Low (tag = 3 codons) |
| Indel | Boundary shift, tag breakage, frame shift | Medium |
| Duplication | Copy of domain or gene вЖТ substrate for neofunctionalization | Medium |
| Inversion | Reverses segment, flips tag orientation | Low |
| **Translocation between genes** | **Fuses domains of different genes вЖТ composite chimera** | Low (main engine of true innovation) |

#### Multi-subunit complexes

Declared by `Structural fold(multimerization_n=k)` + `Surface tag` of self-recognition. Activity вИЭ min of compatible subunit abundances. No additional abstraction.

#### Biomass balance (no molecular tracking)

Continuous variables per lineage: `membrane_integrity`, `wall_integrity`, `dna_progress` вИИ [0,1].
- Produced by enzymes of the lineage (e.g. PBP-like вЖТ wall_integrity), consuming metabolites of Block 6 + ATP
- Degraded by stress (osmotic, antibiotic, lysis)
- `wall_integrity < threshold` during division вЖТ lysis вЖТ reduction of N or extinction

### Block 8 вАФ Selective pressures

#### A. Continuous pressures (reduce fitness)

1. **Out-of-range physico-chemical stress**: temperature/pH/osmolarity deviate from protein ranges (`thermal_stability`, `pH_optimum`) вЖТ progressive drop in kcat (denaturation) вЖТ fitness drops.
2. **Metabolite toxicity**: OвВВ for obligate anaerobes (induces ѕГ-stress, raises ¬µ via DinB-like); HвВВS inhibits cytochromes via competitive binding on Catalytic site of redox; accumulated lactate вЖТ acidifies вЖТ effect (1).
3. **Metabolic deficiencies**: chronically negative ATP balance вЖТ division rate вЖТ 0; specific deficiencies (P вЖТ no DNA, N вЖТ no proteins, Fe вЖТ no cofactors, S вЖТ no sulfur aa). Selection for low Km in oligotrophic niches.

#### B. Discrete death events

4. **Lysis at division**: probability вИЭ `wall_integrity` deficit √Ч osmotic stress.
5. **Phage lysis**: see phage sub-system.
6. **Lethal replication errors**: extreme ¬µ вЖТ fraction of non-viable offspring вЖТ natural upper bound to ¬µ (emergent error catastrophe).

#### C. Non-selective outflow

7. **Dilution rate**: **per-biotope** parameter (free ecological heterogeneity: ponds vs rivers). Constant fraction of population washed away per tick. Dilutes metabolites, signals, free phages. Coupled inflow of fresh nutrients (classic chemostat). Sets carrying capacity, selects for absolute growth rate.

#### D. Competition

8. **Competition for shared resources**: emergent, no dedicated mechanism. Lineages with low Km or more expressed transporters win the shared pool вЖТ others suffer deficiencies.
9. **Bacteriocins**: secreted toxins `[Substrate-binding(target_surface_tag)] [Catalytic(membrane_disruption)]` + "secreted" flag. Target-specific biological warfare between strains вЖТ diversification of surfaces for evasion.

#### Phage sub-system

- **Prophage** = cassette in the chromosome/plasmid: receptor (Surface tag), lysogenic repressor (Regulator output + DNA-binding), viral polymerase, capsid (Structural fold √Ч N), lysis genes (Catalytic membrane_disruption).
- **Lysogenic вЖФ lytic switch**: stress (DNA damage, ѕГ-SOS) degrades the repressor вЖТ induction. Stochastic switch modulated by stress.
- **Free phages**: persistent entities of the biotope, with genome + Surface tag for recognition. Spread via migration (Poisson events вАФ Block 5).
- **Defenses** (covered by existing domains):
  - **Loss-of-receptor**: mutation of the host Surface tag
  - **Restriction-Modification**: enzyme `[Substrate-binding(DNA, palindrome)] [Catalytic(DNA cleavage)]` + methylase with same specificity вЖТ arms race on viral palindromes
- **CRISPR**: omitted from v1 (requires qualitatively different adaptive memory). Reserve for future extension.
- **Co-evolution**: phages mutate like Arkeons (same generative system) вЖТ genuine arms race.

##### Phase 12 вАФ Implementation status

- **Prophage cassettes** (`Genome.prophage()` вЖТ `%{genes, state, repressor_strength}`): the `state :: :lysogenic | :induced` flag makes the commitment to the lytic cycle explicit; `repressor_strength :: float()` (0.0..1.0) modulates sensitivity to stress-driven induction: `p_induction = 0.03 √Ч stress_factor √Ч (1 вИТ repressor_strength)`.
- **Free virions** (`Arkea.Sim.HGT.Virion`): pool of persistent particles per phase, with `genes` (packaged cassette), `surface_signature` (4-codon key for receptor matching), `methylation_profile` (inherited host modification), `decay_age`. Decay via `Phage.decay_step/1` independent of dilution: `decay = 0.20 + 0.05 √Ч decay_age`.
- **`HGT.Phage.lytic_burst/5`**: produces virions into the `phage_pool` of the primary phase (burst size emergent from `ќ£ multimerization_n` of `:structural_fold` domains in the cassette, biologically plausible range 10вАУ500); releases chromosomal fragments into the `dna_pool` of the same phase (substrate for Phase 13 transformation); removes the cassette from the lysed host's genome.
- **`HGT.Phage.infection_step/4`** (`Tick.step_phage_infection/1` in the pipeline): each virion attempts infection on compatible recipients in the same phase. Receptor matching via `surface_signature`. Uniform gating via `HGT.Defense.restriction_check_virion/3`. Lytic/lysogenic decision driven by the cassette's `repressor_strength`: high repression вЖТ lysogeny (child lineage with integrated prophage), low repression вЖТ immediate lysis.
- **`HGT.Defense.restriction_check/3`**: generative R-M gate. Site recognition = `signal_key` of the Catalytic site of a gene containing both `:dna_binding` and `:catalytic_site(reaction_class: :hydrolysis)` (restriction) or `:isomerization` (methylation). Bypass via methylation of the donor sharing a `signal_key` with the recipient's enzyme (Arber-Dussoix host-modification). Probabilistic cleavage (`@cleave_p = 0.70` per vulnerable site) to reproduce the in-vivo baseline escape rate (Tock & Dryden 2005).
- **Extended phenotype** (Phase 12): `restriction_profile :: [signal_key]` and `methylation_profile :: [signal_key]` derived from the composition of genes with co-occurring `dna_binding + catalytic_site`.
- **Audit log mobile_elements**: existing schema; the write path for phage-cycle events (`:phage_infection`, `:rm_digestion`) will be wired in Phase 16 with the `HGT.Channel` behaviour.

##### Phase 13 вАФ Natural transformation

- **Free DNA as substrate** (`Arkea.Sim.HGT.DnaFragment`): pool of fragments per phase (`Phase.dna_pool :: %{fragment_id => DnaFragment.t()}`) with `genes` (chromosome of the lysed cell), `methylation_profile` (inherited host modification), `abundance`, `decay_age`, `origin_lineage_id`. Sources: phage lysis (`HGT.Phage.lytic_burst` deposits the chromosome of the lysed cell); future Phases 14+ will add lysis upon division.
- **Emergent competence** (`Phenotype.competence_score :: 0.0..1.0`): non-zero only if the genome expresses the Phase-13 triad вАФ `:channel_pore` (proxy for ComEC/ComEA) + `:transmembrane_anchor` (proxy for type IV pseudopilus) + `:ligand_sensor` (proxy for ComX / induction signal). Score = `min(1.0, geom_mean(n_channel √Ч n_membrane √Ч n_sensor) √Ч 0.2)`. Effective gating threshold 0.10. A na√ѓve genome вЖТ competence 0.0 (no free uptake).
- **`HGT.Channel.Transformation.step/4`** (in the `Tick.step_hgt` pipeline, between conjugation and induction): for each competent recipient, iterates over the `dna_pool` of the phase, computes `p_uptake = 0.0006 √Ч competence √Ч fragment.abundance` (cap 0.20), R-M gating via `HGT.Defense.restriction_check/3` with `fragment.methylation_profile`, positional homologous recombination (allelic replacement: gene at position *i* of the donor вЖТ position *i* of the recipient if both indices exist).
- **Self-uptake excluded**: `fragment.origin_lineage_id == recipient.id` вЖТ rejected (deterministic no-op, prevents inflation).
- **Conservation**: each gate event (digestion, uptake, or homology rejection) consumes one unit of `fragment.abundance`. Fragments at abundance 0 are pruned.
- **Decay**: `Phase.dilute/1` applies the dilution rate to fragments as for free virions; implicit ageing via `decay_age` (prepared for Phase 18 refinement if accelerated decay is needed).
- **Audit log**: existing schema; `:transformation_event` events will be wired in Phase 16 with the `HGT.Channel` behaviour.

##### Phase 14 вАФ Toxicity, elemental constraints, continuous biomass

- **Toxicity** (`Metabolism.toxicity_factor/2` + `Phenotype.detoxify_targets`): three toxic metabolites (`:oxygen`, `:h2s`, `:lactate`) have an encoded `(threshold, scale)`. For each unprotected metabolite, contribution = `1 - max(0, [met] - threshold)/scale`, clamped to `0..1`; contributions compose multiplicatively. Generative detoxify enzyme: a gene with co-occurring `:substrate_binding(target=metabolite)` + `:catalytic_site(reaction_class: :reduction)` вЖТ `detoxify_targets вИЛ metabolite` вЖТ full bypass for that target. Reproduces catalase (OвВВ), sulfide oxidoreductase (HвВВS), lactate dehydrogenase (lactate) as emergent traits.
- **Elemental constraints** (`Metabolism.elemental_factor/3`): for each tracked essential element (P/N/Fe/S) (`@elemental_metabolites`), score = `min(1.0, uptake / floor)` with `floor = 0.001 √Ч abundance`. Geometric mean вЖТ global factor. **Only elements that the phenotype actively seeks (`:substrate_binding` present)** count as a constraint; a na√ѓve genome is not penalized for nutrients it does not seek (the prototype does not model cellular recycling). Factor in `0.0..1.0` applied only to biosynthesis (biomass progress), not to respiration.
- **Continuous biomass** (`Lineage.biomass :: %{membrane, wall, dna}`): three components in `0.0..1.0` (default 1.0 = intact founder). Per-tick:
  - `progress`: scaled by `(atp_yield/50) √Ч tox`. Per-component capability: membrane вИЭ `n_transmembrane/5`, wall вИЭ `n_transmembrane/5` (PBP-like proxy), DNA вИЭ `repair_efficiency √Ч elemental`.
  - `decay`: osmotic shock outside the tolerance band ¬±200 mOsm/L (membrane + wall decay); elemental shortage (DNA decay `(1-elemental) √Ч 0.05`).
- **`Arkea.Sim.Biomass.lysis_probability/1`**: per-component pressure below threshold (membrane 0.30, wall 0.40, DNA 0.25) composes as max вЖТ lysis probability in `0..1`.
- **Pipeline** (`Tick`): new `step_biomass` after `step_metabolism` (reuses `state.uptake_by_lineage` to avoid recomputing on an already-drained pool); new `step_lysis` after `step_environment` and before `step_pruning` (Bernoulli trial per lineage, reduces abundance per phase by `floor(count √Ч p)`).
- **State**: `BiotopeState.uptake_by_lineage` added to share uptake from `step_metabolism` to `step_biomass`.
- Phase 17 will use the same biomass as substrate for `error_catastrophe` and SOS coupling.

##### Phase 15 вАФ Xenobiotics and RAS (Resistance Antibiotic Selection)

- **Xenobiotic catalog** (`Arkea.Sim.Xenobiotic`): atom вЖТ entry map with `target_class`, `kd`, `mode` (`:cidal | :static | :mutagen`), `degradable_by_hydrolase`. v1 has `:beta_lactam` (target `:pbp_like`, Kd 10.0, cidal, degradable). Additive catalog вАФ new entries do not break anything existing.
- **Xenobiotic pool** (`Phase.xenobiotic_pool :: %{xeno_id => float}`): concentration on metabolite_pool scale. Dilution in `Phase.dilute/1` like the other pools.
- **Three emergent resistance mechanisms** (all generative from the 11 existing domains):
  1. **Target absence**: `Phenotype.target_classes :: %{atom => float}` (proxy abundance for `:pbp_like`, `:dna_polymerase_like`, `:ribosome_like`, `:membrane`). PBP-like = co-occurrence `:transmembrane_anchor + :catalytic_site`. Lineage without the target вЖТ intrinsic resistance.
  2. **Enzymatic degradation**: `Phenotype.hydrolase_capacity :: float` = count of genes with `:substrate_binding + :catalytic_site(reaction_class: :hydrolysis)` (ќ≤-lactamase-like). `Xenobiotic.degradation_amount/3` = `k_deg √Ч conc √Ч hydrolase √Ч abundance`, capped at conc.
  3. **Efflux pump**: `Phenotype.efflux_capacity :: 0.0..1.0` = co-occurrence `:transmembrane_anchor + :channel_pore + :energy_coupling + :substrate_binding` capped at 1.0 (max protection 90%).
- **Binding model** (`Xenobiotic.bound_fraction/2`): saturating Hill-like `bound = [drug_intracellular] / (Kd + [drug_intracellular])`. Survival per drug = `1 - bound √Ч mode_severity √Ч target_abundance`. Mode severity: cidal 0.95, static 0.50, mutagen 0.0 (Phase 17 will wire the mutagen to SOS ¬µ).
- **Pipeline** (`Tick.step_xenobiotic`): between `step_metabolism` and `step_biomass`. (1) for each lineage applies `Xenobiotic.survival_factor/3` to its `atp_yield_by_lineage` post-toxicity. (2) for each degradable xenobiotic, sums the degradation across all lineages and subtracts it from the pool. Skipped when all pools are empty (no overhead in absence of drugs).
- **Player intervention** (`Arkea.Sim.Intervention :xenobiotic_pulse`): `kind: :xenobiotic_pulse, phase_name:, xenobiotic_id: :beta_lactam, dose: 50.0` injects a pulse into the pool. Default dose 50.0.
- **End-to-end RAS test**: seed scenario with a hydrolase-bearing population (ќ≤-lactamase-like) + ќ≤-lactam pulse shows progressive detoxification of the pool over time (test in `xenobiotic_test.exs`).
- **Audit log**: `:xenobiotic_pulse` events recorded as intervention payload (existing). Phase 16 will wire per-lineage drug-driven death events.

##### Phase 16 вАФ Advanced plasmid traits and transduction

- **Refactor `Genome.plasmids`** (`Genome.plasmid()` map): each plasmid now carries `genes`, `inc_group`, `copy_number`, `oriT_present` derived generatively from the genes themselves. `Genome.normalize_plasmid/1` is idempotent on already-formed maps and automatically wraps legacy gene lists. `Genome.new/2` and `Genome.add_plasmid/2` accept both forms.
- **`inc_group`** (`0..@inc_group_modulus-1` with `@inc_group_modulus = 7`): hash of the plasmid genes' codons modulo 7. `Genome.add_plasmid/2` applies **automatic displacement**: adding a plasmid with the same `inc_group` as a resident replaces the resident (Novick 1987 incompatibility, simplified). `set_plasmids/2` for wholesale replacement without displacement.
- **`copy_number`** (`1..@max_copy_number` with `@max_copy_number = 10`): `1 + count(:dna_binding domains)` clamped. Tighter repressor binding вЗТ stricter replication control вЗТ higher steady-state copy number (San Mill√°n & MacLean 2018).
- **Burden scaled by copy_number** (`Tick.compute_growth_deltas_v5`): `plasmid_burden = ќ£ (length(plasmid.genes) √Ч plasmid.copy_number √Ч 0.3)`. High-copy plasmids amplify the cost; the gene-dosage benefit will be wired in Phase 17 via kcat scaling.
- **`oriT_present`**: `true` if at least one gene of the plasmid carries an intergenic block `orit_site`. `Intergenic.transfer_probability_multiplier/3` consumes the flag as a donor-side boost.
- **Generalised transduction** (`Virion.payload_kind :: :phage | :generalized_transduction | :specialized_transduction`): `HGT.Phage.lytic_burst/5` with probability `@transduction_probability = 0.05` mis-packages a random chromosomal gene into a transducing virion (`@transducing_burst_fraction = 0.03` of the burst size). The virion travels through `phage_pool` like a normal phage virion; in `infection_step/4` the branch on `payload_kind` reroutes delivery towards positional allelic replacement (no lysogenic integration). Specialised transduction is declared in the schema (`:specialized_transduction`) but the mis-excision path will be wired in Phase 17.
- **Behaviour `Arkea.Sim.HGT.Channel`**: new formal interface `step/4 :: {lineages, phase, children, rng}` + callback `name/0`. Implemented by `HGT.Channel.Transformation` (Phase 13) and `HGT.Phage` (Phase 12 + 16). Conjugation (`HGT.step/4`) retains the legacy signature for now; behaviour conformance deferred to Phase 17.
- **Audit log**: the `audit_log` schema accepts atom-typed events via the catchall `Atom.to_string`; new Phase 16+ event types (`:transformation_event`, `:transduction_event`, `:plasmid_displaced`, `:rm_digestion`) can flow transparently. Explicit emission of these events in the channels will be wired in Phase 17 together with `:error_catastrophe_death` and `:bacteriocin_kill`.

##### Phase 17 вАФ SOS, error catastrophe, operons, bacteriocins

- **`Lineage.dna_damage :: 0.0..@dna_damage_max`** (cap 5.0): biologically coherent accumulator of lineage DNA damage. Per-tick increment normalised per-cell via `Mutator.dna_damage_increment/4`: `¬µ_baseline √Ч (replications/abundance) √Ч (1 - repair_efficiency)`, with a √Ч1.5 boost if SOS is already active (DinB-like compounding). Fixed decay `Mutator.decay_damage/1` `0.10/tick`. New `Tick.step_dna_damage` between `step_cell_events` and `step_hgt`.
- **SOS response** (`Mutator.sos_active?/1`): `dna_damage вЙ• @sos_active_threshold = 0.50`. Generative-only effects:
  - `Mutator.mutation_probability/3` amplifies ¬µ √Ч `@sos_mutation_amplifier = 4` (active mutator strain).
  - `HGT.induction_step` multiplies the prophage induction probability by `@sos_induction_amplifier = 3` (RecA-mediated repressor cleavage). Replaces the ATP-deficit-only trigger from Phase 12 with the ATP-stress + DNA-damage convergence.
- **Error catastrophe** (`Mutator.error_catastrophe_lethality/2`): conforms to the Eigen quasispecies threshold. For `¬µ_per_cell √Ч genome_size > 1`, `p_lethal = 1 - (1 - share)^genome_size` where `share = (product - 1) / genome_size`. Wired in `Tick.attempt_spawn`: if the roll falls below p_lethal, the offspring is non-viable (the parent pays the replicative cost, no new lineage). RNG skip when `p_lethal == 0` to preserve deterministic paths in canary scenarios.
- **Operons** (`Gene.operon_id :: binary | nil`): new data-model field (default `nil`). Genes sharing the same `operon_id` form an operon вАФ runtime semantics of coordinated kcat scaling and shared ѕГ-factor deferred to a follow-up; Phase 17 ships the struct shape so that builders and mutators can already tag co-transcribed genes.
- **Bacteriocins** (`Arkea.Sim.Bacteriocin`): narrow-spectrum proteinaceous warfare (Riley & Wertz 2002). Producer = genome carrying (a) a gene containing `:substrate_binding + :catalytic_site(:hydrolysis) + :transmembrane_anchor` *and* (b) at least one `:surface_tag` (immunity marker). The dual requirement prevents self-destructive suicide producers. Dynamic pool:
  - `Phase.toxin_pool :: %{producer_lineage_id => float}` indexed by producer (for immunity matching).
  - Secretion: `producer.abundance √Ч @secretion_per_cell (=0.0001)` per tick.
  - Damage: every non-immune lineage (no shared `:surface_tag` with the producer) loses `@damage_rate (=0.005) √Ч concentration` from `biomass.wall`, capped at `@max_damage_per_pool (=0.05)` per pool. Lethality routes through the existing `Tick.step_lysis` вАФ no parallel death mechanism.
  - Self-immunity: the producer skips its own pool. Cross-immunity: lineages with a matching shared `:surface_tag` skip the producer's pool.
  - Standard dilution via `Phase.dilute/1`.
- **`Tick` pipeline**: new `step_bacteriocin` between `step_signaling` and `step_expression`; new `step_dna_damage` between `step_cell_events` and `step_hgt`. Error catastrophe folded into `attempt_spawn`.
- **Audit log**: existing `audit_log` schema accepts `:sos_active`, `:error_catastrophe_death`, `:bacteriocin_kill` via the catchall `Atom.to_string`. Explicit emission of these event types remains wireable per-channel when the `HGT.Channel` behaviour is extended (Phase 18+).

##### Phase 18 вАФ Cross-feeding closure, biofilm, Poisson mixing

- **Cross-feeding closure** (`Metabolism.byproducts/1` + `Metabolism.compute_byproducts/1`): per-substrate stoichiometric table that closes the C/N/S/Fe/HвВВ cycles. Glucose вЖТ acetate + COвВВ + HвВВ; acetate вЖТ COвВВ; lactate вЖТ acetate + COвВВ + HвВВ; SOвВД¬≤вБї вЗМ HвВВS; NHвВГ вЗМ NOвВГвБї; COвВВ вЗМ CHвВД; CHвВД вЖТ COвВВ. Fractional coefficients (yield per unit of substrate consumed), not strict mass-conservation вАФ the goal is qualitative cycle closure that permits emergent syntrophy (sulfate-reducer вЖТ sulfide-oxidiser, fermenter вЖТ hydrogenotrophic methanogen). Wired into `Tick.process_phase`: by-products are added to `phase.metabolite_pool` *after* consumption, so the donor cannot immediately re-uptake its own waste.
- **Biofilm capability** (`Phenotype.biofilm_capable? :: boolean()`): true when the genome expresses both a surface anchor (any `:surface_tag`) and a multimeric structural protein (`:structural_fold` with `multimerization_n вЙ• 2`, proxy for the EPS matrix). The two-prong requirement prevents false positives: a single adhesin without a structural scaffold does not form a biofilm.
- **Biofilm dilution relief** (`Tick.step_environment` Phase 18 update): biofilm-capable lineages get a discount of `@biofilm_dilution_relief = 0.5` on the dilution rate of their phase (50% extra residence relative to planktonic cells at the same physical rate). Per-lineage discount applied multiplicatively: `effective_rate = base_rate √Ч (1 - 0.5) = 0.5 √Ч base_rate`.
- **Mixing event Poisson** (`Tick.step_mixing_event`): Bernoulli roll per tick against `@mixing_event_probability = 1.0e-4`. On a hit: uniform redistribution of lineage abundances across phases (analogue of storm / sediment turnover / intestinal mixing) + homogenisation of metabolite_pool and signal_pool. No-op on single-phase biotopes. RNG-frugal: the roll consumes a single `:rand.uniform_s` per tick. No actor / payload (it is a natural disturbance), distinct from the player-triggered `:mixing_event` intervention that remains available via `Arkea.Sim.Intervention`.
- **`Tick` pipeline**: new `step_mixing_event` between `step_lysis` and `step_pruning` (mixing after deaths are counted, before cap enforcement). Final sequence: `step_metabolism вЖТ step_xenobiotic вЖТ step_biomass вЖТ step_signaling вЖТ step_bacteriocin вЖТ step_expression вЖТ step_cell_events вЖТ step_dna_damage вЖТ step_hgt вЖТ step_phage_infection вЖТ step_environment вЖТ step_lysis вЖТ step_mixing_event вЖТ step_pruning`.

### Block 9 вАФ Quorum sensing & signaling

Cell-cell signaling is a central mechanism of bacterial ecology. Integrated in v1 without new domain types.

#### Signal molecules as fourth environmental class of the biotope

| Class | Role |
|---|---|
| Metabolites (13) | Substrates, acceptors, nutrients, products |
| Xenobiotics | target_class + affinity + mode |
| **Signal molecules** | Cell-cell communication, **no metabolic role** |
| Phage particles | Predation, transductional HGT |

Signals have concentration, decay, dilution like metabolites, but are not consumed by metabolic reactions.

#### Generativity: no catalog

Each signal has a **signature** (4D vector encoded in the parameter codons of the synthase). Mutations of the synthase вЖТ slightly different signals (analogy: the different AHLs in the real world).

#### Signaling genes (covered by the existing domains of Block 7)

| Function | Composition |
|---|---|
| Signal synthase | `[Substrate-binding(precursor)] [Catalytic(reaction_class=signal_synthesis, signature=X)]` |
| Signal receptor | `[Ligand sensor(target_signature=Y, threshold)] [DNA-binding] [Regulator output]` |
| Signal transporter | `[Substrate-binding(signature)] [Channel/pore] [Transmembrane]` |

Minimal extensions to the Block 7 taxonomy:
- Addition of `signal_synthesis` to the reaction_class of `Catalytic site`
- Generalization of `sensed_metabolite_id` to `sensed_chemical_id` (metabolites **or** signals)

#### Signal-receptor specificity: continuous matching

```
binding_affinity = exp(вИТ ||signal_signature вИТ receptor_target||¬≤ / ѕГ¬≤)
```

Coevolution required between synthase and receptor. Wide `ѕГ` вЖТ eavesdropping; narrow `ѕГ` вЖТ private communication. Communicative divergence = natural speciation mechanism.

#### Quorum behavior (emergent)

Density-dependence emerges from concentrations:
- Low density вЖТ low production вЖТ signal below threshold вЖТ program OFF
- High density вЖТ signal above threshold вЖТ program ON

Typical programs under QS control (evolutionary decision or player design): bacteriocins, biofilm formation, conjugation, phage virulence, sporulation-like.

#### Migration, dilution, decay

Signals follow the same rules as metabolites for dilution and migration between biotopes. Spontaneous decay (parameter per signature) by default fast вАФ signals are unstable.

#### Free extensions (emergent from the framework)

- **Inter-species cross-talk** (eavesdropping)
- **Quorum quenching**: signal hydrolases of others' signals `[Substrate-binding(signature)] [Catalytic(hydrolysis)]` вЖТ destroys competitors' QS
- **Phage signaling**: phages that release signals upon lysis (call, warning) вАФ emergent if it survives evolutionarily

#### Operational caps

- Maximum distinct coexisting signals per biotope: ~50вАУ100 (with merging if too similar) вАФ tuning parameter
- Signature dimensionality: **4D** (default; supports thousands of dialects)

#### Geometric topology

- **2D abstract planar** graph (navigable map)
- Each biotope: coordinates `(x, y)` + 3вАУ5 nearest neighbors
- **Long-range bridges**: ~1вАУ2% of arcs вЖТ inter-regional HGT, connected world
- **Clustered geographical distribution**: archetypes form *zones* (hydrothermal region, marshy region, marine region, etc.). At zone borders intermediate biotopes emerge вЖТ natural ecological gradients. Bridges connect distant zones rarely.

#### Arcs: weights and directionality

- `edge_weight` вИЭ 1/geographical distance
- `biotope_compatibility(A, B)` derived from distance in the environmental profile (T, pH, osmolarity) вЖТ a thermophile does not establish in the cold lake
- **Per-archetype directionality**: symmetric for ponds (lakes, bogs), asymmetric for flowing (springs=upstream, sediments=downstream)
- Along the arcs flow (with independent scaling): lineages, free phages, metabolites, signals

#### Allocation and colonization (multi-biotope, cap 3)

Cap **3 biotopes per player** = 1 home + up to 2 colonized.

- New player: chooses Tier 1 archetype вЖТ home biotope in the corresponding zone
- Tier 2 and Tier 3 unlocked by progression
- **Colonization mechanism** (relying on migration of Block 5 + lineages of Block 4):
  1. Player's lineages migrate into neighboring biotopes
  2. If a lineage originated in the home reaches **abundance вЙ• threshold** in a **wild adjacent** biotope, maintained for **вЙ• N ticks**, the biotope is **claimed**
  3. **First-to-threshold** in case of competition between players
  4. Only wild biotopes are colonizable (no claim on others' biotopes вАФ consistent with no-direct-PvP)
- **Emergent expansion from evolution**: specialists colonize their own zone easily, generalists more widely but with less specialization. For distant zones: cross-tolerance or fortunate bridge edge.

#### Abandoned biotopes вЖТ wild *(default to be confirmed)*

Player inactive for вЙ• T days вЖТ biotopes return to **wild**:
- Population persists and continues to evolve autonomously
- Intervention rights revoked
- Biotope colonizable again (also by the original player if they return)

**Simultaneously resolves**: Arkeon offline-state behavior (Block 1) + churn/saturation of the network (wilds are colonization reserves for newcomers).

#### World sizing

| Resource | Steady-state quantity |
|---|---|
| Fixed natural biotopes (at launch) | ~200вАУ400 distributed over the 8 archetypes and zones |
| Player home biotopes | 1 per active player |
| Player colonized biotopes | 0вАУ2 per active player |
| Wild biotopes (ex-inactive players) | growing over time, colonization reserve |

**Simulation cost (estimate)**: cap 1,000 lineages/biotope √Ч ~10вБі total biotopes at steady state = 10вБЈ lineage updates per tick. With tick every 5 min (see Block 11), well within the reach of a moderate cluster.

### Block 11 вАФ Temporal sizing

#### Fundamental unit

- **1 tick = 5 real minutes = 1 reference generation** (ideal Arkeon under perfect conditions)
- Real lineages vary: stressed ones may require 5вАУ10 ticks to divide; in bloom 1 tick. The "reference generation" is just a unit of kinetic calibration.
- "Slow game" choice: privileges evolutionary depth and multi-session gameplay over fast feedback.

#### Scale of evolutionary phenomena

| Real time | Tick / generations | Observable by player |
|---|---|---|
| 5 min | 1 | Isolated mutations; response to acute stress |
| 30 min | 6 | Visible drift; first variants |
| 1 hour | 12 | Visible directional selection; effects of first interventions |
| Session 2h | 24 | Consolidated selective events |
| Session 5h | 60 | Partial use case (visible resistances) |
| 1 day | ~288 | Complete resistance; phage-host co-evolution started |
| 1 week | ~2,000 | Complete co-evolution; colonization; initial speciation |
| 1 month | ~8,600 | Macro-evolution: change of dominant archetype in zone; communicative speciation |

The "antibiotic resistance in 100 generations" use case of Block 5 requires **~8 real hours** вЖТ multi-session, consistent with persistent MMO.

#### Tick kinetics (execution order)

For each biotope, per tick:
1. Metabolic balance (steady-state proto-FBA on intracellular pools + environment)
2. Gene expression (ѕГ-factor, riboswitch, regulators evaluated; levels updated)
3. Cellular events (divisions, deaths, lysis, mutations вЖТ new lineages)
4. HGT (conjugation/transformation/transduction between lineages of the biotope)
5. Environmental events (metabolite/signal decay, dilution)
6. Pruning (lineages below threshold вЖТ locally extinct, preserved in the global tree)

Between ticks, global coordinated step:

7. Inter-biotope migration (flows on arcs: lineages, phages, metabolites, signals)

#### Server: walltime = simulation clock

- Server processes **exactly 1 tick every 5 minutes wall-clock**
- **Always uniform time** вАФ no local fast-forward (consistent with unitary MMO)
- All players share the same "now"
- Server restart: tick stays synchronized with wall-clock; on restart, resumes from the tick corresponding to the current time

**Parallelization**: biotopes are independent during steps 1вАУ6 (intra-tick) вЖТ massively parallelizable. Step 7 requires synchronization between neighboring arcs вЖТ globally coordinated step.

#### Persistence

- **Full DB snapshot every 10 ticks (50 real minutes)**: state of all biotopes
- **Write-ahead log** intra-snapshot for every change (new lineage, HGT, mutation, player intervention) вЖТ complete recovery in case of crash
- **Historical phylogenetic tree** written incrementally with periodic decimation (Block 4)

#### Client-side polling

- Overview view: server polling every 15вАУ30 seconds
- Zoom view on biotope: polling 5вАУ10 seconds
- Notable events (dominant extinctions, colonizations, QS peaks, phage mass lysis) вЖТ asynchronous push notifications

#### Computational cost (revised with 5-min tick)

- 10вБі biotopes √Ч 1,000 lineages cap = 10вБЈ lineage updates per tick
- 1 tick = 300 sec of available walltime вЖТ ~33k updates/sec required
- At ~1 ¬µs per lineage update (optimistic) = 10s of CPU per tick вЖТ ample margin
- With realism 10√Ч worse, still 100s out of 300s вЖТ **3√Ч margin**
- At steady state with 10вБі biotopes вЖТ **2вАУ5 node cluster** sufficient with shard per region

#### Perceptual latency of interventions

Player actions (antibiotic, nutrients, inoculum, plasmid) вЖТ applied at the next tick. **Maximum latency 5 minutes**. Effects measurable in 5вАУ30 ticks (25вАУ150 minutes). Cadence consistent with "slow" strategic MMO.

### Block 12 вАФ Micro/macroscale boundary

#### Phase-based model

Each biotope is subdivided into **2вАУ3 phases** (conceptual sub-environments, not spatially discretized). The simulation is authoritative **at the phase level**, not at the 2D position level.

#### Number of phases per archetype (variable)

| Archetype | Phases (authoritative) |
|---|---|
| Oligotrophic lake | `surface (oxic)`, `water_column (semi-mixed)` |
| Eutrophic pond | `surface (oxic)`, `water_column`, `sediment (anoxic)` |
| Marine sediment | `interface (gradient)`, `bulk_sediment (anoxic)` |
| Hydrothermal vent | `vent_core (hot)`, `mixing_zone (gradient T)` |
| Acid drainage | `acid_water`, `mineral_surface (biofilm-friendly)` |
| Methanogenic bog | `surface_oxic`, `peat_core (anoxic)` |
| Heterogeneous soil | `aerated_pore`, `wet_clump (anoxic)`, `soil_water` |
| Saline estuary | `freshwater_layer`, `mixing_zone`, `marine_layer` |

Each phase has:
- Its own pools of metabolites, signals, free phages
- Its own subsets of lineages (each lineage has abundance per phase, possibly zero in some)
- Its own environmental parameters (T, pH, osmolarity) вАФ may diverge from biotope-average
- Its own dilution rate (e.g. surface high, sediment very low)

#### What is authoritative vs visualization

| Aspect | Authoritative | Pure visualization |
|---|---|---|
| Lineage abundances per phase | вЬЕ | вАФ |
| Metabolite / signal / phage concentrations per phase | вЬЕ | вАФ |
| Environmental parameters per phase | вЬЕ | вАФ |
| 2D individual positions of "dots" | вАФ | вЬЕ derived from phase distribution |
| Biofilm shapes, colonies, animations | вАФ | вЬЕ procedural from phase density |
| Interpolated concentration heatmap | вАФ | вЬЕ from phase concentration |
| Phage lysis waves | вАФ | вЬЕ animation of mass lysis event |

#### Phase preference: encoded in the genome

Covered by the domains of Block 7, without new types:
- **Adhesion вЖТ biofilm/surface**: `Surface tag(adhesin)` + `Structural fold(rigid)`
- **Motility вЖТ water_column**: flagellar subunits (`Structural fold(filamentous)` + `Energy-coupling`)
- **OвВВ tolerance вЖТ oxic phases**: presence of protective genes (catalase-like)
- **Physical stress tolerance вЖТ extreme phases**: thermal_stability/pH_optimum of proteins

Distribution between phases computed per tick as a function of lineage phenotype and phase parameters. Emergent, not player choice.

#### Phase-to-phase migration

Migration (Block 5) flows **at the phase level**: a cell in `surface` of A migrates preferentially toward `surface` of B. `biotope_compatibility` generalized to `phase_compatibility(A.phase_x, B.phase_y)`.

Consequence: a "biofilm-ist" lineage moves little (anchored), a "planktonic" one diffuses rapidly. Consistent with real biology.

#### Player interventions

Granularity: **biotope-wide or phase-level**, never precise 2D coordinate.

Examples:
- "Antibiotic in the sediment of my pond" вАФ phase-level
- "Inoculum plasmid in surface" вАФ phase-level
- "Mixing event" вАФ **available strategic action**: temporarily remixes the phases (homogenizes concentrations and populations), with metabolic/resource cost for the player. Allows maneuvers like "I expose the anaerobes to OвВВ" or "I dilute a toxic metabolite throughout the volume"
- "Click a specific dot in the 2D view" вАФ **not available** (position is not authoritative)

#### 2D visualization: procedural rendering

- Phase abundances determine screen regions (e.g. upper band = surface, lower band = sediment)
- The "dots" represent fractions of lineages, randomly positioned within the region of their phase
- Rendering density вИЭ N of the lineage (with procedural clustering to simulate colonies/biofilms)
- Coloring by phenotypic cluster (Block 4)
- Events (mass lysis, selective sweep) as procedural animated effects above the base rendering

The rendering is **derived** from the authoritative state, does not influence it.

#### Additional cost

10вБі biotopes √Ч 2.5 average phases √Ч 1k lineages = ~2.5 √Ч 10вБЈ lineage-phase updates per tick. With 300s walltime, ample margin (вЙ• 3√Ч) relative to the budget of Block 11.

### Block 13 вАФ Residual anti-griefing

#### Identified vectors

Despite the no-direct-PvP choice (Block 3), indirect ways remain to harm other players:

| # | Vector | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Plasmid burden flooding | Costly/non-useful plasmid released вЖТ migration + HGT вЖТ weakens recipients |
| 2 | Hyper-virulent phage release | Hyper-aggressive phage вЖТ free particles migrate (Poisson) вЖТ infect others |
| 3 | Bacteriocin shotgun | Bacteriocins evolved for common surface tags вЖТ spread via migration |
| 4 | Signal spam / quorum quenching | Signal flooding or anti-signal hydrolases вЖТ disrupts others' QS |
| 5 | Resource depletion in colonized wild | Hyperconsumption of metabolites вЖТ deficiency for others' migrants |
| 6 | Colonization grabbing | Rapid colonization of adjacent wilds to wall in |
| 7 | Abandoned-biotope seeding | "Trojan" pre-evolution + abandonment вЖТ permanent harmful wild |
| 8 | Multi-account coordination | Multiple coordinated accounts to amplify attacks |

#### Mitigations already covered by the design

Most vectors are self-neutralized by previous blocks:

- **Dilution + decay** (Blocks 3, 8, 9, 10): all effects decay with distance from the release point. Attacks on players in distant zones impossible.
- **Plasmid cost** (Block 5): burden plasmid selected against вЖТ extinct in few generations if not advantageous. **Vector #1 self-neutralized**.
- **Phage-host co-evolution** (Block 8): RM and loss-of-receptor defenses evolve naturally. No sustainable "invincible phage". **Vector #2 exhausts itself**.
- **Target-match bacteriocins** (Block 8): effective only against specific Surface tags вЖТ diversification of surfaces (Block 7) makes them ineffective at scale. **Vector #3 limited**.
- **Real time as bottleneck** (Block 11): all interventions require wall-clock. High human cost of griefing.
- **Colonization cap 2** (Block 10): walling-in scale limited.
- **Distance in the network** (Block 10): clustered zones + rare bridge edges вЖТ cross-zone attacks must traverse intermediate biotopes.

#### Explicit residual mitigations

**A. Cooldown on colonization**
After a successful colonization: **24h real cooldown** before the next one. Limits "grabbing" without penalizing organic expansion (1вАУ2 colonizations/week is reasonable scope). *Mitigates vector #6.*

**B. Audit log of mobile elements (origin tracking)**
Each mobile element (plasmid, prophage) carries an in-game-invisible metadata with `origin_lineage_id` and `origin_biotope_id`. Allows ops systems to detect abusive patterns (e.g. "phage X originated by player Y appears in N biotopes attached to player Z within 1 week"). *Mitigates vectors #1, #2, #7.*

**C. Claim sustainability**
Beyond the abundance threshold (Block 10): the claim holds only if the lineage's population grows or remains stable for **N ticks post-claim**. If it collapses вЖТ claim revoked, biotope returns to wild. Penalizes opportunistic grabs, rewards real adaptation. *Mitigates vector #6.*

**D. Intervention budget per biotope**
Regenerative cap on player interventions: e.g. **1 major intervention every 30 real minutes per biotope**. Not punitive in normal play (interventions are sparingly used anyway), shuts down automated abuse. *Mitigates vectors #1, #2, #4 in flooding mode.*

#### "No action needed" documented

**E. Pre-evolution + abandonment (vector #7)**: wilds follow normal ecology. Natural selection erodes purely harmful traits without benefit to the carrier (penalizes them by metabolic/genomic cost). **Structural mitigation**: the ecological design prevents the persistence of "harmful-without-benefit" traits. No explicit action necessary.

**F. Multi-account / bot (vector #8)**: **operational** topic (registration, IP detection, behavioral analysis), not of ecological design. Must be addressed in the operational plan but out of scope for the biological model.

### Block 14 вАФ Technological stack

#### Runtime decision: BEAM/Elixir

**Sim core + orchestration**: Elixir + OTP. The strengths of BEAM align with Arkea's needs:
- Fault tolerance via supervision trees ("let it crash + recover") for 24/7 sim
- Distributed cluster out-of-the-box via `libcluster` + `:net_kernel`
- Lightweight process model (~3 KB/process): one GenServer per biotope is natural and scales
- Phoenix Channels + Presence + PubSub for real-time push to client
- Hot code reload for deploy without downtime on persistent MMO

**Known weakness**: pure numerical performance (10вБї100√Ч slower than Rust). **Future mitigation**: extract the hot kernels (proto-FBA, parameter computation from codons, batch kinetic update) as **NIF Rust** via Rustler/Zigler when profiling justifies it. **For the prototype: pure Elixir**, optimize only after profiling real bottlenecks.

#### Complete stack

### Block 15 вАФ Integral use case (tabletop stress test)

Walk-through of a multi-week campaign that traverses all 14 previous blocks in a continuous scenario. Purpose: validate internal coherence before implementation.

#### Setup

- **Anna** (Tier 1 account): home `STAGNO-A` (Eutrophic pond archetype) in marshy zone. Gram-positive Arkeon, thick wall, facultative aerobic heterotroph + fermenter.
- **Bartolomeo** (Tier 2 account): home `ESTUARIO-B` (Saline estuary archetype) in coastal zone. Halophilic Gram-negative Arkeon.
- **Topology**: zones connected by a rare bridge edge (~5 graph hops away).
- **Time**: 30 real days вЙИ 8,640 ticks вЙИ 8,600 generations.

#### Week 1 вАФ Settlement and emergent biofilm

- Anna inoculates 10 cells in STAGNO-A; exponential growth to 10вБі; emergent phase distribution (70% water_column / 25% surface / 5% sediment)
- Point drift produces 30вАУ40 daughter lineages; mild selection; active pruning
- Anna feeds extra-glucose in `surface` (intervention budget вИТ1) вЖТ QS threshold activated вЖТ emergent biofilm in surface; distribution shifts to 50/35/15

[Blocks: 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13]

#### Week 2 вАФ Stress, mutator, resistance

- Anna introduces ќ≤-lactam-like (0.5√Ч MIC) in water_column; mass lysis 10вБґ вЖТ 10вБі; surface biofilm and sediment protected
- ѕГ-stress activates DinB-like (¬µ √Ч 50); point mutation on PBP-like shifts Km вЖТ partial resistance fixed
- Dose raised to 1√Ч MIC; duplication of an efflux transporter + neofunctionalization вЖТ MIC 4√Ч; removed, balanced polymorphism by cost

[Blocks: 4, 5, 7, 8, 12]

#### Week 3 вАФ Prophage, defenses, first colonization

- Free phage arrives from wild (Poisson); direct lysis + lysogeny
- Defenses evolve: loss-of-receptor (with cost) + Restriction-Modification (DNA hydrolase + methylase tandem from translocation)
- Anna colonizes LAGO-W (planktonic sub-lineage reaches 30% for >100 ticks вЖТ claim, 24h cooldown triggers)
- Anna induces communicative separation in LAGO-W: synthase drifts вЖТ signal outside ѕГ of the receptor of STAGNO-A вЖТ partial communicative speciation

[Blocks: 3, 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, 13]

#### Week 4 вАФ The indirect attack

- Bartolomeo releases a non-conjugative burden plasmid from ESTUARIO-B (audit log: origin_lineage_id, origin_biotope_id)
- Plasmid cost вЖТ local spread stabilizes at ~5%
- Migration along bridge edge: dilution + selection through 5 intermediate hops вЖТ fraction < 0.1% on arrival in Anna's zone
- In LAGO-W only passive transformation (non-conjugative) вЖТ < 0.05%
- Selection against completes elimination in 500 generations; no damage to Anna; principle E ("no action needed") confirmed

[Blocks: 5, 8, 10, 13]

#### Week 5 вАФ Macro-evolution

- STAGNO-A: dominant founder lineage; forest at 2,000 lineages (cap 1,000 + pruning, decimation of historical tree)
- LAGO-W: chimera by translocation fuses `Substrate-binding(Fe¬≥вБЇ)` + `Catalytic(reduction)` вЖТ new ferric reductase вЖТ niche in sub-oxic phases; **composite innovation**
- The two strains genetically and communicatively distinct, HGT-linked by migration

[Blocks: 4, 7, 9]

#### Design gaps emerged

Unlike the first use case (8 gaps), the integral walk-through **does not reveal serious inconsistencies**. Three minor considerations, of balancing/implementation:

1. **Intervention budget vs exploratory sessions**: 1 action/30 min may be tight for those who experiment intensively. Possible accumulable pool up to N stored actions. Balancing decision.
2. **Phase distribution for newborns**: a just-born lineage inherits the parent's distribution (snapshot at the instant of birth), recomputes on the next tick. To be clarified in implementation.
3. **Cap 1,000 in just-colonized wild**: resident wild lineages stay and follow the same rules. Wildness = flag of non-control, no different mechanical treatment. To be confirmed in implementation.

#### Conclusion

**Internally coherent** design. The 14 blocks integrate into a continuous scenario that produces biologically plausible phenomena: resistance, biofilm, prophages, RM, colonization, speciation, composite innovation, failure of an indirect attack. The 3 remaining points are refinements, not gaps. **Ready for the implementation phase.**
